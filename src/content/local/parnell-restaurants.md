# Best Restaurants in Parnell, Auckland 2026

Parnell is Auckland's oldest suburb — Victorian and Edwardian architecture, Parnell Road's collection of galleries and boutiques, a dining scene that has served the suburb's well-heeled residents for fifty years. The area has a reputation for conservative dining that is increasingly inaccurate.

The restaurants that matter in Parnell in 2026 are not the heritage rooms coasting on their past. They are the venues that have taken the suburb's stable, affluent, returning customer base and used it to build something genuinely excellent. Parnell diners expect a certain standard and the best restaurants on the strip have decided to exceed it rather than meet it.

---

## Non Solo Pizza

**What it is:** Parnell Road Italian — one of Auckland's most beloved neighbourhood Italian restaurants, going for decades without becoming lazy.

**Why it matters:** Non Solo Pizza has the difficult quality of a genuinely good neighbourhood restaurant: it is reliable without being boring, familiar without being complacent. The handmade pasta is the anchor. The pizza (which is excellent, despite the name suggesting more is on offer) is wood-fired and properly made. The Italian wine list is extensive and the floor team can guide you through it.

**What to order:** The fresh pasta — whatever preparation is on today. The wood-fired pizza (do not skip it out of misplaced loyalty to the pasta). The tiramisu, which has been on the menu forever because it is correctly made.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Reliable, fair Parnell Road Italian pricing.

**The room:** Two levels, the street-side terrace, a room that has fed multiple generations of Parnell residents.

---

## Cibo

**What it is:** A Parnell institution — the restaurant that has defined serious dining in the suburb for over twenty years.

**Why it matters:** Cibo has built its reputation on consistency and on a menu that takes New Zealand produce seriously without the farm-to-table performance. The wine list is excellent — one of the best in the eastern suburbs. The room is one of Auckland's most elegant in the Italian sense: warm, unhurried, a space that suits a long dinner.

**What to order:** The pasta, which is among the best in the eastern suburbs. The seafood, which changes with availability. The daily specials, which reflect what the kitchen received that morning.

**Price range:** $$$. Parnell fine dining pricing — appropriate for the quality and the room.

**The room:** Converted heritage building. One of Auckland's genuinely beautiful dining rooms.

---

## Rydges Auckland Rotisserie

**What it is:** The Hotel Rydges rooftop rotisserie restaurant on the Parnell/CBD border — a destination in its own right, with panoramic Auckland harbour views and a serious rotisserie programme.

**Why it matters:** The rotisserie format is simple and the quality is not. Whole animals slow-cooked on the rotisserie, sourced from named New Zealand suppliers, served with the harbour view that reminds you Auckland is a city on the water. The view and the roast are both worth the trip.

**What to order:** Whatever is on the rotisserie today. The complementary sides — roasted vegetables, house-made sauces. The Auckland harbour at sunset from the terrace.

**Price range:** $$$. Hotel restaurant pricing, fair for the experience.

---

## Pasadena

**What it is:** A Parnell Road restaurant with a California-influenced menu — seasonal, vegetable-forward where it suits, honest about its sourcing.

**Why it matters:** Pasadena has filled a gap in Parnell's dining: the casual-but-serious restaurant that functions as a neighbourhood regular but produces food at a level that draws people from other suburbs. The brunch is excellent. The dinner, which gets less attention, is equally worth it.

**What to order:** The seasonal salads and vegetable dishes are the kitchen at its most distinctive. The protein dishes are excellent. The brunch at any hour.

**Price range:** $$. Among the better-value serious restaurants in Parnell.

---

## The Conservatory

**What it is:** Parnell Road café-restaurant in a heritage conservatory setting — genuinely beautiful room, with food to match.

**Why it matters:** The Conservatory occupies one of Auckland's most distinctive dining spaces — a Victorian-era glasshouse on Parnell Road with natural light that makes everything look better. The kitchen has recognised that the room sets an expectation and built a menu worth it: fresh, seasonal, carefully sourced.

**What to order:** Brunch, which is the kitchen at its peak. The seasonal plates. The baked goods, which are made in-house.

**Price range:** $$. Heritage setting, neighbourhood pricing.

**The room:** The conservatory itself — natural light, plants, heritage glass. One of Auckland's most photographed dining rooms, for the right reasons.

## Eating in Parnell

**Parnell Road is compact.** The restaurant strip runs from Parnell Rise to Heather Street — all walkable, all visible. You can assess your dinner options while walking to your booking.

**The suburb suits a long, unhurried dinner.** Parnell's dining culture is not high-energy or competitive. The rooms are calmer than Ponsonby, the service more attentive, the pace slower. Build your evening to take advantage of this.

**Book for the serious rooms.** Cibo and Non Solo Pizza fill on weekends. Book ahead for Friday and Saturday. Midweek is generally accessible.

**Parnell to Newmarket is walkable.** The two suburbs are five minutes apart and the dining between them — Parnell on one side, Newmarket's Broadway strip on the other — gives you options for a post-dinner walk and dessert somewhere different.
