# Best Restaurants in Christchurch 2026

The 2011 earthquakes destroyed the old Christchurch hospitality landscape in the most literal sense. When the rebuild came, it came with a generation of chefs who chose to stay and build something new rather than leave for Auckland or Melbourne. The result is a dining scene that is younger, more risk-willing, and more interesting than the one it replaced.

Canterbury's food geography helps: some of the best lamb in the world, salmon from the Waitaki, stone fruit from the central plains, Akaroa salmon, venison from the high country. The chefs who chose Christchurch chose it in part because of what the region can put on a plate.

---

## Fugu

**What it is:** Christchurch's most ambitious restaurant — omakase Japanese dining in the post-earthquake city that has become a national reference point for the format.

**Why it matters:** The omakase format requires a level of trust from the diner that most New Zealand cities outside Auckland have struggled to generate. Christchurch has generated it. Fugu's chefs source with precision — Japanese technique applied to New Zealand seafood and produce from Canterbury farms — and the results have placed the restaurant in serious company nationally.

**What to order:** The omakase menu. This is not a restaurant where ordering à la carte gives you the experience.

**Price range:** $$$$. Omakase pricing. The kind of meal you think about for months.

**Book ahead:** Essential. The counter seats are limited and they fill.

---

## Gatherings

**What it is:** Christchurch's leading farm-to-table restaurant — a tasting menu format built around Canterbury produce and the farm relationships of head chef Giulio Sturla.

**Why it matters:** Gatherings has become the restaurant that serious food travellers go to in Christchurch — and there are more of them than you might expect. The tasting menu format changes constantly with the season and with what the named farms are producing. The kitchen is technically precise and produces plates that look better than they need to and taste better than they look.

**What to order:** The tasting menu. Vegetable-forward dishes are the kitchen's strongest statement — Canterbury's produce makes this easier than in most cities.

**Price range:** $$$$. Tasting menu pricing. Worth it.

---

## Twenty Seven Steps

**What it is:** A Cathedral Square area restaurant that has survived Christchurch's post-earthquake upheaval and produced consistent fine dining for over a decade.

**Why it matters:** Twenty Seven Steps occupies a Victorian heritage building in the post-rebuild city — a rare thing — and has matched the room with food that understands what it means to be a serious restaurant in a city that is still re-establishing its dining geography. The New Zealand-European menu is executed with precision.

**What to order:** The Canterbury lamb. The seasonal fish. The desserts, which are among the most accomplished in Christchurch. The wine list has strong regional New Zealand representation.

**Price range:** $$$–$$$$. Fine dining in the city's upper tier.

---

## King of Snake

**What it is:** Southeast Asian cooking in the city's Heritage Precinct — a restaurant that has become one of Christchurch's most reliably excellent rooms for food that is genuinely interesting.

**Why it matters:** King of Snake does not approximate Southeast Asian cuisine. The kitchen cooks from an understanding of the traditions — Thai, Vietnamese, Malay, Chinese — that produces dishes with depth rather than the generic "Asian fusion" that plagues the category in New Zealand's smaller cities.

**What to order:** The sharing format is correct for this food. The kingfish ceviche with Southeast Asian influences. The whole fish. The curry preparations. The cocktail list is seriously good.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. One of the best-value serious restaurants in Christchurch.

**The room:** A heritage building interior with warmth and buzz. One of Christchurch's best rooms for a group.

---

## Lyttelton — The Nearby Alternative

Christchurch's satellite dining scene in Lyttelton (fifteen minutes over the hill) deserves mention separately. Lyttelton's London Street has produced some of the most interesting restaurants and bars in the Canterbury region, and the Saturday market is the best farmers market in the South Island.

**Roots Restaurant**, Lyttelton: a fine dining operation from Giulio Sturla (same chef as Gatherings) with a different format — tasting menu, extremely seasonal, one of the most decorated restaurants in New Zealand. Book months ahead.

---

## The Bicycle Thief

**What it is:** A New Brighton area restaurant that has become one of Christchurch's most beloved neighbourhood venues — the kind of place that keeps its suburb believing that living away from the centre is worth it.

**Why it matters:** The Bicycle Thief demonstrates what a neighbourhood restaurant can be when it takes its food as seriously as a destination restaurant. The rotating menu is seasonal and sourced locally. The room is warm and the service is genuine rather than performed.

**What to order:** The seasonal menu — whatever is listed first. The wine list has intelligent regional selections. The desserts are made in-house.

**Price range:** $$. Among the best neighbourhood restaurant value in Christchurch.

## Eating in Post-Rebuild Christchurch

**The dining geography has changed.** The old Christchurch restaurant geography — centred on the CBD restaurant strip — no longer exists. The post-rebuild city is spread across multiple precincts: Victoria Street, Oxford Terrace, the heritage precinct, New Brighton. Research before you go.

**The rebuild produced good architecture.** Some of Christchurch's best new restaurants occupy buildings that were specifically designed for them in the post-earthquake rebuild. The dining rooms in the heritage precinct and in new-build Victorian-inspired spaces are genuinely worth experiencing.

**The road to Lyttelton is worth driving.** The fifteen-minute drive over the Port Hills to Lyttelton is one of New Zealand's great short drives. The dining at the other end — Roots especially — is worth travelling for from further than Christchurch.

**Book the serious venues ahead.** Fugu, Gatherings, and Roots (Lyttelton) book out weeks in advance, particularly for Saturday dinner. Christchurch's food-aware population has limited inventory at these rooms.
