# Best Restaurants in Newmarket, Auckland 2026

Newmarket is Auckland's shopping suburb. Broadway is one of the country's busiest retail strips, and the suburb's food reputation has historically been an extension of that: places to eat between purchases, cafes serving shopping-centre traffic, restaurants that capture the passing trade.

That picture is incomplete. Behind and around the Broadway strip, Newmarket has developed a dining scene that rewards the diners who look for it rather than settle for the obvious. The suburb's affluent residential base and its position between Parnell and Remuera have produced a cluster of restaurants that take quality seriously.

---

## Rosé + Wild

**What it is:** A Newmarket wine bar and restaurant with a strong natural and minimal-intervention wine focus — one of Auckland's better evening destinations outside the Ponsonby cluster.

**Why it matters:** Rosé + Wild has arrived with a list that takes wine seriously and food that earns its place alongside it. The natural wine focus gives it a coherent identity in a suburb that has needed one in the wine bar category. The small plates format is well executed and the kitchen knows what it is doing.

**What to order:** Ask what is exciting on the wine list this week — the list rotates and the team selects carefully. The cheese and charcuterie board. The seasonal small plates from the kitchen.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Wine bar pricing for the quality.

---

## Faro

**What it is:** A Korean restaurant in Newmarket that has become one of Auckland's most important venues for serious Korean cooking.

**Why it matters:** Faro has done something rare: built a Korean restaurant that attracts the non-Korean dining public to the same degree as the Korean community in Auckland. The banchan preparation is serious. The barbecue programme uses quality New Zealand beef and pork. The bibimbap is made correctly.

**What to order:** The barbecue table if your group is three or more. The bibimbap. The kimchi jjigae (kimchi stew). The banchan spread — the quality of these small dishes is the marker of a serious Korean kitchen.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Fair for the quality and the format.

**The room:** Korean restaurant energy — full, loud on weekends, a room that works for groups.

---

## Harvest Kitchen

**What it is:** A Newmarket restaurant with a farm-to-table approach and a serious commitment to sourcing from named New Zealand farms.

**Why it matters:** Harvest Kitchen fills the gap in Newmarket's dining that Ponsonby's farm-to-table restaurants fill for the isthmus: a restaurant where the menu is determined by what arrived that morning rather than by what is on the printed card. The execution is confident and the seasonal vegetable preparations are particularly strong.

**What to order:** The daily specials. The vegetable dishes from whatever is in season. The protein from the named farm suppliers.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Well-positioned for the quality.

---

## Antoine's

**What it is:** A Parnell Road restaurant technically on the Newmarket–Parnell border that has been one of Auckland's most loved fine dining institutions since 1973.

**Why it matters:** Antoine's has operated through every trend in Auckland dining since the 1970s without needing to change because the French-influenced classical cooking was always the point. The room is formal in a way that Auckland has largely stopped doing. The food is excellent in a way that never became unfashionable.

**What to order:** The French classics — the duck, the rack of lamb, the crème brûlée that has been on the menu since 1973 for good reason.

**Price range:** $$$$. Auckland's old-school fine dining pricing — appropriate.

**The room:** Old Auckland. White tablecloths, silver service, a room that does not apologise for what it is.

---

## Burger Burger

**What it is:** Auckland's best burger restaurant — on Nuffield Street in Newmarket, with a commitment to quality produce that makes it a destination rather than a convenience.

**Why it matters:** Burger Burger sources its beef from named New Zealand farms, makes its sauces and condiments in-house, and bakes its buns fresh. The result is a burger that would be notable even in Sydney or Melbourne, and is the best of its kind in Auckland.

**What to order:** The Double Burger. The fried chicken option if available. The onion rings. The thick milkshakes, which are made properly.

**Price range:** $$. The best-value quality burger in Auckland east of Ponsonby.

**The room:** A Nuffield Street building with counter seating and a design that takes the burger seriously without taking itself too seriously.

## Eating in Newmarket

**The Broadway trap.** The restaurants directly on Broadway are primarily serving shopping traffic. The better dining in Newmarket is on the side streets — Nuffield Street, the Mortimer Pass laneway, the blocks toward Parnell. Navigate accordingly.

**Newmarket to Parnell is five minutes on foot.** The two suburbs share enough of a dining geography that an evening can span both — drinks in one, dinner in the other.

**Book the wine bar on weekends.** Rosé + Wild fills quickly for weekend evening sessions. Book or arrive early.

Several Newmarket venues list midweek specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — a suburb that runs on shopping-hour traffic often has its best dining specials on Tuesday and Wednesday evenings.
