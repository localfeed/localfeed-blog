# Best Bars in Te Aro, Wellington 2026

Wellington has the best bar scene in New Zealand. This is not a controversial opinion among people who drink seriously in New Zealand. The combination of the city's compact walkability, its creative industries culture, its university population, and its genuinely competitive bar scene has produced something that larger cities — Auckland, Brisbane, Perth — have not replicated.

Te Aro is where it lives. Cuba Street is the spine, but the lanes around it — Tory Street, Dixon Street, Allen Street, the blocks toward Willis — carry a density of genuinely excellent bars that rewards exploration.

---

## Golding's Free Dive

**What it is:** Cuba Street's anchor for serious drinking — a bar that has held the corner of Cuba and Vivian for years and has used that position to build one of Wellington's best craft beer programmes.

**Why it matters:** Golding's is the bar that Wellington's craft beer culture circles around. The taps rotate through the best New Zealand and international craft breweries, and the team running the selection knows the difference between a token craft tap and a curated list. The bar also has genuinely good spirits and functions as a cafe in the morning — the dual-mode authenticity extends to both services.

**What to order:** Ask what is new on the taps. The Wellington and Wairarapa craft breweries are usually represented. The natural wine section for those not drinking beer. The bar food is honest.

**Price range:** $$. Among the best-value serious drinking in Wellington.

---

## Matterhorn

**What it is:** A Cuba Street institution going back to the 1960s — a bar and restaurant that has been reinvented while maintaining its cultural position at the centre of Wellington's nightlife.

**Why it matters:** Matterhorn operates at the highest level as both a cocktail bar and a restaurant simultaneously. The cocktail programme is genuinely serious — the bartenders know classic technique and the seasonal ingredient approach produces drinks that are worth the price. The food matches the bar.

**What to order:** A spirit-forward cocktail — the Negroni is a reference point. The Matterhorn seasonal cocktail menu. The food: the bar plates and the main courses are equally worth ordering.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Cuba Street premium bar pricing.

**The room:** One of Wellington's most distinctive bar interiors. The long bar, the booth seating, the particular quality of a room that has been getting it right for a long time.

---

## Library Bar

**What it is:** Te Aro's reference point for the serious cocktail format — a bar with one of Wellington's deepest spirits selections and the knowledge to do something interesting with it.

**Why it matters:** Library Bar has carved out a specific identity in Wellington's bar landscape: the bar you go to when you want to drink something precisely made from a serious spirits list. The books-and-spirits aesthetic is not an affectation — the selection is genuinely curated and the bartenders know what is in the bottles.

**What to order:** Ask what is interesting in the whisky selection. The cocktails made with the seasonal ingredients. The amaro and digestif selection for after dinner.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Specialty cocktail bar pricing.

---

## Hashigo Zake

**What it is:** Te Aro's most serious craft beer bar — underground, dark, with a taps and bottles selection that has been setting the Wellington craft beer standard for fifteen years.

**Why it matters:** Hashigo Zake is the bar where Wellington's craft beer culture has its most serious expression. The tap list rotates through breweries that are doing interesting things. The bottle and can selection goes further than the taps. The events programme (tap takeovers, brewer visits, release nights) has shaped Wellington's craft beer community.

**What to order:** Ask what is rarest or most interesting on the board today — the team knows the context behind every tap. The food (simple, well-executed) works with the beer.

**Price range:** $$. Craft beer pricing — fair for the quality.

**The room:** Underground. Dark. The kind of bar that knows what it is and has no interest in being something else.

---

## Rogue & Vagabond

**What it is:** A Garrett Street craft beer and bar venue that has built its following through a combination of good beer, a flexible room, and a dog-friendly policy that Wellington's population finds irresistible.

**Why it matters:** Rogue & Vagabond is the neighbourhood bar version of the serious craft beer experience. Less curated than Hashigo Zake, more approachable, with a garden bar that functions as Wellington's best outdoor drinking space on good-weather days.

**What to order:** The rotating craft beer taps. The cider selection. The bar food — the pies and the bar plates are made properly.

**Price range:** $$. Neighbourhood bar pricing.

**The room:** Indoor and outdoor, dog-friendly garden, a layout that works for any group size.

---

## Hawthorn Lounge

**What it is:** A Te Aro cocktail bar with one of Wellington's most considered spirits selections and an approach to cocktail making that takes the classics seriously.

**Why it matters:** Hawthorn Lounge sits in the serious cocktail category without the pretension that damages some bars in that position. The spirits selection is deep in American whiskey and rum. The cocktail list balances classics and seasonal originals. The bar team knows the list and the history behind it.

**What to order:** The classic cocktails made from the whiskey section. The seasonal specials. Ask the bartender what they are currently exploring — the good bars in this category always have something interesting off-menu.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Wellington cocktail bar pricing.

## Drinking in Te Aro: How It Works

**Walk everything.** Golding's to Matterhorn to Library to Hashigo Zake to Hawthorn Lounge is a route that most Te Aro bar evenings take in some variation. None of it requires transport.

**Start early.** Wellington's bars at 5:30pm–7pm are a different experience from 11pm. The bartenders are less stretched, the rooms are less crowded, and the cocktails get more attention.

**The weather is part of it.** Wellington's famous wind makes the transition from the outdoors to a warm bar one of the city's reliable pleasures. Embrace the contrast.

**Eat well before the serious bars.** Te Aro's restaurant scene (Logan Brown, Shepherd, Ortega) surrounds the bar scene geographically. Build an evening that combines both — dinner at 6:30pm, bars from 9pm, and you have experienced Wellington properly.

Several Te Aro bars list their early-evening specials and quiet-night offers on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the city's flat white and craft beer culture extends to a willingness to post genuine deals rather than platform-forced discounts.
