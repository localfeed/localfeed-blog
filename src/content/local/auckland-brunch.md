# Best Brunch in Auckland 2026

Auckland has a claim on brunch culture that stretches back further than the Instagram era that commercialised it. The all-day café, the weekend eggs, the flat white alongside — this is a format Auckland was building before the global brunch moment arrived. What changed is the standard: Auckland's best brunch is now genuinely excellent, not just reliable.

The venues below represent Auckland's brunch at its most worthwhile — across the suburbs, across formats, across the range from neighbourhood institutions to destination cafes.

---

## Orphans Kitchen (Ponsonby)

**What it is:** Ponsonby Road's most important restaurant also runs the most considered brunch service on the strip.

**Why it matters:** Orphans Kitchen's brunch reflects the same sourcing commitment as the dinner menu — the bread is made in-house, the eggs are from named farms, the seasonal produce determines what appears on the plates rather than the reverse. The weekend brunch here is worth booking in advance.

**What to order:** The toast with cultured butter is one of Auckland's most ordered brunch items. The egg preparations, which change with what the kitchen received this week. Whatever vegetable-forward preparation the kitchen is leading with.

**Price range:** $$$. Worth it.

---

## Rosie (Ponsonby)

**What it is:** A Ponsonby Road neighbourhood café with a brunch menu that sets the standard for what a neighbourhood café should deliver.

**Why it matters:** Rosie executes the weekend brunch template correctly: quality coffee from a serious roaster, food that changes with the season, a room that is warm without being crowded. The regulars come every week because the food earns them.

**What to order:** The seasonal brunch plates. The flat white, which is consistently among the best on Ponsonby Road. The sweets from the cabinet.

**Price range:** $$. Well-priced neighbourhood brunch.

---

## Best Ugly Bagels (Ponsonby Central)

**What it is:** Wood-fired Montreal-style bagels — Auckland's most specific brunch proposition.

**Why it matters:** Best Ugly solved the Auckland bagel problem (previously: there was not a good one) by bringing the Montreal tradition — dense, chewy, wood-fired rather than boiled — to Ponsonby Central. The schmear is house-made. The smoked salmon is properly sourced. The queue on Sunday morning is real and worth it.

**What to order:** The smoked salmon bagel with cream cheese and everything seasoning. The plain bagel with house-made jam. A bag to take to the park — the correct Auckland Sunday activity.

**Price range:** $–$$. Honest.

---

## Dizengoff (Ponsonby)

**What it is:** An Israeli-influenced café going since 1994 — one of Auckland's most consistent brunch institutions.

**Why it matters:** Dizengoff has maintained its identity — the shakshuka, the sabich, the specifically Israeli café sensibility — for thirty years without compromising for the Auckland mainstream. The shakshuka is the best version in the city. The coffee is serious.

**What to order:** The shakshuka. The sabich on a good day. The French toast for something less specific.

**Price range:** $$. Thirty years of fair pricing.

---

## Kokako (Grey Lynn)

**What it is:** Auckland's most celebrated specialty roaster runs its best café on Surrey Crescent in Grey Lynn.

**Why it matters:** Kokako is the Auckland roaster that has set the ethical and quality standard for New Zealand specialty coffee. The Grey Lynn café is where you drink it freshest. The brunch food alongside it reflects the same sourcing ethic.

**What to order:** The pour-over from the current seasonal single-origin. The brunch plates that change with the season and the Grey Lynn Saturday farmers market.

**Price range:** $$. Specialty café pricing.

---

## Winona Forever (Grey Lynn)

**What it is:** A natural wine bar and restaurant that also runs a weekend brunch worth going to specifically.

**Why it matters:** Winona Forever's brunch is the wine-bar brunch done correctly — a menu that takes the cooking seriously, sources locally, and treats the morning meal with the same attention as the evening. The natural wine programme extends to a breakfast amaro if you are inclined.

**What to order:** The seasonal brunch plates — the kitchen changes this weekly. The orange juice, fresh-pressed.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Wine-bar café pricing.

---

## The Federal Delicatessen (Auckland CBD)

**What it is:** Al Brown's New York Jewish deli on Federal Street — one of Auckland's most beloved all-day restaurants.

**Why it matters:** The Fed has taken the deli format — pastrami, bagels, eggs, matzo ball soup — and executed it with quality ingredients and a kitchen that does not cut corners. The Saturday and Sunday brunch at The Fed is a specific Auckland experience: the clatter of the deli, the smell of pastrami, a Reuben that has no right to be as good as it is.

**What to order:** The Reuben sandwich. The eggs and pastrami hash. The bagel with cream cheese and salmon. The blintzes.

**Price range:** $$. Deli pricing that is honest for the quality.

---

## Licorne (Ponsonby)

**What it is:** A French-inspired café on Ponsonby Road with a pastry programme that is among the best in Auckland.

**Why it matters:** Licorne does not try to be everything. It bakes very well, makes excellent coffee, and produces a brunch experience that is specifically French in its orientation — the croissants are laminated correctly, the pain au chocolat is made with real chocolate, the tarts are worth ordering over a sweet tooth impulse.

**What to order:** The croissant. Whatever tart is in the cabinet. The eggs sur le plat for a savourys. The café au lait.

**Price range:** $$. French café pricing.

## Auckland Brunch: How to Navigate It

**Ponsonby is the centre.** The highest concentration of excellent brunch venues in Auckland is a fifteen-minute walk along Ponsonby Road and its adjacent streets. Most of the best venues are within this area.

**Saturday at 8:30am or 10:30am.** The 9am–10am window is when every good Ponsonby café is at capacity. Arrive early or arrive late. Midweek brunch (Tuesday–Friday) is the best-kept secret in Auckland café culture.

**Book for the popular ones.** Orphans Kitchen brunch needs a booking. Dizengoff and Best Ugly do not take bookings and queue accordingly.

**Grey Lynn for the regulars experience.** The Kokako and Grey Lynn farmers market combination on Saturday morning is one of Auckland's authentic food experiences — less polished than Ponsonby, equally excellent.

Several Auckland brunch venues list their quiet-weekday morning specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the best brunch on Tuesday at 9am is a different, better experience than the same café at 10am Saturday. Worth checking.
