# Best Restaurants in Wanaka 2026

Wanaka is smaller than Queenstown and operates at a different register. The adventure tourism is still there — the skiing, the kayaking, the same mountain backdrop — but the town has a quieter confidence that Queenstown, with its three million annual visitors, cannot maintain. That quietness produces better dining.

The restaurants in Wanaka are not built for throughput. They are built for a population of permanent residents, semi-permanent seasonal workers, and visitors who chose Wanaka specifically because they wanted something less crowded. The result is a dining scene that is more relaxed, more local, and in several cases more interesting than what the better-known town forty minutes away delivers.

---

## Francesca's Italian Kitchen

**What it is:** Wanaka's finest restaurant — Italian cooking in a Helwick Street setting that has become the town's destination for a significant dinner.

**Why it matters:** Francesca's has built its reputation on the things that Italian cooking requires: handmade pasta, quality sourcing, a kitchen that takes its technique seriously and does not overcomplicate it. The wood-fired preparations are a strength. The Italian wine list is deep in the southern Italian and Sicilian regions that complement the food. The room is warm and unhurried.

**What to order:** The handmade pasta — the kitchen's most consistent strength. The wood-fired protein preparations. The desserts, which are made in-house. The Italian wine selection.

**Price range:** $$$. Wanaka's best restaurant at appropriate pricing.

**Book ahead:** Essential for weekend dinner. Wanaka's dining scene is small and the good rooms fill.

---

## Ritual Café

**What it is:** Wanaka's specialty coffee anchor — a café that has been setting the town's coffee standard for years and whose brunch has become a destination in its own right.

**Why it matters:** Ritual has done what good specialty cafes do: built a loyal following through consistent quality and a sourcing approach that is honest. The coffee is from serious roasters. The food is made in the kitchen from local Central Otago produce. The brunch plates reflect seasonal availability rather than a fixed menu.

**What to order:** The flat white — the reference point for what the café can do with espresso. The seasonal brunch plates. Whatever the kitchen is most excited about this week.

**Price range:** $$. Specialty café pricing.

---

## The Cow Shed

**What it is:** A Wanaka café and restaurant in the Hawea Flat area — outside town, but a destination for the produce-focused cooking it produces.

**Why it matters:** The Cow Shed occupies a converted farm building and has built a menu around the agriculture immediately surrounding it. Central Otago lamb, regional produce, dairy from the Wanaka basin — the food reflects where the restaurant is in a way that feels genuine rather than gestural.

**What to order:** The lamb preparation — sourced from the regional farms and cooked with the attention it deserves. The seasonal vegetable dishes. The stone fruit desserts in summer, which are among Central Otago's most distinctive seasonal pleasures.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Farm-restaurant pricing.

---

## Bistro Gentil

**What it is:** A Wanaka bistro with a menu that reflects both European technique and the Central Otago produce geography — the most complete dining option in the town for a proper dinner.

**Why it matters:** Bistro Gentil has built its following through consistency and through a wine list that gives serious space to Central Otago's Pinot Noir and Riesling producers. The food — European in structure, seasonal in content — is executed with a care that matches the wine list.

**What to order:** The Pinot Noir pairing with the lamb. The seasonal fish from the South Island coast. The cheese selection, which features Central Otago and South Island producers.

**Price range:** $$$. Wanaka's second serious restaurant option.

---

## Pemberton's

**What it is:** A Wanaka restaurant and bar on Ardmore Street — the town's most accessible serious dining option, with food that goes above what the casual format suggests.

**Why it matters:** Pemberton's operates as the restaurant that Wanaka's hospitality workers eat at on their nights off — a useful indicator of genuine quality. The menu is seasonal and the kitchen is serious. The wine list has good Central Otago representation.

**What to order:** The daily specials. The beef, which comes from the regional farms. The desserts.

**Price range:** $$. Accessible Wanaka dining.

## Eating in Wanaka

**Drive from Queenstown.** The forty-five minute drive from Queenstown to Wanaka via Crown Range Road (or the longer but more dramatic Cardrona route) is one of the great South Island drives. The Crown Range in summer is a reason to make the drive in itself.

**Central Otago wine is the context.** Wanaka is surrounded by Central Otago Pinot Noir country. Rippon Vineyard is five minutes from town — the organic estate with the Lake Wanaka view is one of New Zealand's most beautiful winery visits. The restaurant scene reflects this wine geography.

**Rippon Vineyard.** Not a restaurant, but the cellar door experience at Rippon — with the lake and the mountains visible behind the vines — is one of New Zealand's most complete winery experiences. The Pinot Noir is exceptional. Book ahead for harvest events.

**Book ahead in ski season.** Wanaka's dining scene is small. Francesca's and Bistro Gentil fill quickly from July through August. Book before you arrive.
