# Best Bars in Wellington 2026

Wellington's bar scene is the best in New Zealand. This is a claim that can be defended across categories: craft beer density, cocktail programme quality, natural wine curation, bar-adjacent food quality. The city has more excellent bars per capita than Auckland or Christchurch by a significant margin.

The reasons are structural. Wellington's walkable geography concentrates bars within a small area and creates competition that raises quality. The university and creative industries population creates a drinking public that is genuinely interested in what it is drinking. The city's relationship with craft brewing — Epic, Garage Project, and a generation of Wellington brewers — has been formative for the national craft beer movement.

This is where to drink in New Zealand's best bar city.

---

## Garage Project Taproom

**What it is:** The Aro Valley taproom for Garage Project — one of New Zealand's most important craft breweries, where the full range of their production is available on tap.

**Why it matters:** Garage Project has been shaping New Zealand's craft beer culture since 2011 from their Aro Street premises. The taproom is where you drink the full range — the session beers, the experimental releases, the seasonal specials, the barrel-aged rarities — in the building where they are brewed. For anyone serious about New Zealand craft beer, this is the destination.

**What to order:** Ask what is on that cannot be found in bottles or cans — the taproom-only releases and the experimental drafts are the reason to come here rather than a bottle shop. The food (bar plates) is simple and honest.

**Price range:** $$. Brewery taproom pricing.

---

## Golding's Free Dive

**What it is:** Cuba Street's most important bar — fifteen years of running the most respected craft beer programme on Wellington's main drag.

**Why it matters:** Golding's has held its position through consistent curation and through a bar team that treats the selection as a genuine project rather than a stock list. The taps rotate through Wellington's best breweries and national producers doing interesting things. The bottle and can selection goes further. The natural wine and spirits hold their own.

**What to order:** Whatever is newest and most interesting on the taps — the board changes and the staff can explain it. The natural wine selection for those preferring wine. The bar food is honest and well-priced.

**Price range:** $$. The best value for quality in Wellington's serious bar scene.

## Library Bar

**What it is:** A Te Aro cocktail bar with one of the deepest spirits selections in the country — the reference point for Wellington cocktail culture.

**Why it matters:** Library Bar has built its position through investment in the spirits selection and the knowledge to use it. The whisky range is exceptional — American, Scotch, Japanese, Irish represented with depth rather than tokenism. The cocktail list combines classics with originals that reflect what the bar team is currently interested in.

**What to order:** A spirit-forward classic made from the whisky selection. Ask about the bartender's current seasonal project. The amaro selection for after dinner.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Cocktail bar pricing.

## Hashigo Zake

**What it is:** Te Aro's most serious craft beer bar — underground, dark, uncompromising about what it stocks.

**Why it matters:** Hashigo Zake is the bar that Wellington's craft beer community uses as a reference point. The tap list reflects genuine curation — rarities, imports, the best of New Zealand's small breweries in their most interesting formats. The events programme (brewer visits, release nights, tap takeovers) has shaped Wellington craft beer culture for fifteen years.

**What to order:** Ask what is unusual on the board today. The team knows the provenance of every tap and can explain why it is there. The bar food is minimal and correct.

**Price range:** $$. Serious craft beer bar pricing.

---

## Matterhorn

**What it is:** A Cuba Street institution that has been Wellington's benchmark cocktail bar for over fifty years — reinvented without losing its position.

**Why it matters:** Matterhorn does something very few bars achieve: it is simultaneously a serious cocktail bar, a serious restaurant, and a serious music venue, without doing any of them poorly. The cocktail programme is built on classical technique and seasonal ingredients. The food alongside it is excellent. The room is one of Wellington's most photographed interiors.

**What to order:** The Negroni as a reference point — how a bar makes a Negroni tells you what it thinks about cocktails. The seasonal cocktail menu from the current programme. The food: the bar plates and the restaurant menu are both worth it.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Wellington's premium cocktail bar.

---

## The Rogue & Vagabond

**What it is:** Garrett Street craft beer and bar — a dog-friendly venue with one of Wellington's best garden bars and an honest drinking culture.

**Why it matters:** Rogue & Vagabond occupies the approachable end of Wellington's serious bar spectrum: the craft beer selection is good, the atmosphere is relaxed, the garden is the best outdoor drinking space in the Te Aro area. The pub food is honest — pies, bar plates, things that work alongside beer.

**What to order:** The rotating craft beer taps. The cider selection. The bar food. The garden bar on a Wellington summer afternoon.

**Price range:** $$. Neighbourhood bar pricing.

---

## Bar Medici

**What it is:** An Italian aperitivo bar in the Wellington CBD — the most authentic expression of the Italian bar tradition in New Zealand.

**Why it matters:** Bar Medici has built its identity around the Italian aperitivo hour — Campari, Aperol, Negroni, Spritz, served with the kind of bar food (cicchetti, charcuterie, olives) that the Italian bar tradition pairs them with. In a bar culture dominated by craft beer and cocktails, Bar Medici offers something genuinely different and genuinely good.

**What to order:** An Aperol Spritz or a Negroni. The Campari and soda. The bar food from the cicchetti selection. The Italian wine list.

**Price range:** $$. Aperitivo bar pricing — the food often comes with the drinks.

## Drinking in Wellington: What to Know

**Walk everything.** The bars above are almost all within a fifteen-minute walk of each other. Wellington's evening geography is pedestrian and the walking between bars is part of the experience.

**The weather makes bars better.** Wellington's wind and rain drive people inside and into warm bars. This is not a hardship — it produces an indoor bar culture that is among the most atmospheric in New Zealand.

**Start early.** Wellington bars at 5pm are a different experience from Wellington bars at 11pm. The Garage Project taproom in the late afternoon, Library Bar at 6pm for a cocktail before dinner — this is Wellington at its best.

Several Wellington bars list their early-evening and midweek specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the city's bar culture extends to genuine offers rather than platform-mandated discounts.
