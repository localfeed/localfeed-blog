# Best Restaurants in Takapuna, Auckland 2026

Takapuna spent years being unfairly dismissed by the Auckland food establishment — a suburb where the dining was fine for the North Shore but where serious food people crossed the bridge for dinner. That dismissal is outdated.

The Takapuna dining scene in 2026 has a concentration of genuinely excellent restaurants that would not embarrass Ponsonby. The beach, the flat walkable geography, the affluent residential base, and a generation of Aucklanders who moved to the North Shore but refused to lower their food standards have produced a suburb that now serves its own culinary identity.

---

## Ika Seafood Bar & Grill

**What it is:** Takapuna's best seafood restaurant — on Lake Road, with a menu that reflects the genuine commitment to product quality that the name suggests.

**Why it matters:** Ika ("fish" in te reo Māori) has built its reputation on sourcing and execution. The daily fish is genuinely daily — what arrived from the boat or the market that morning — and the kitchen treats it with a respect that makes the difference between good seafood and great seafood. The crudo and raw preparations are particularly strong.

**What to order:** The daily whole fish — whatever the kitchen recommends. The ceviche and crudo. The shellfish when in season (Bluff oysters, scallops, clams). The wine list has good white and sparkling selections to match the food.

**Price range:** $$$. Justified by the product quality.

---

## Estuary

**What it is:** A Takapuna restaurant that has become the suburb's reference point for a special occasion dinner — elegant, serious, and worth the North Shore drive.

**Why it matters:** Estuary operates in the space between a formal restaurant and a relaxed neighbourhood bistro, and gets the balance right. The menu is European-influenced, seasonal, and built around relationships with suppliers that produce better ingredients than the generic food service approach. The wine list is one of the best on the North Shore.

**What to order:** The set menu is the best expression of what the kitchen can do. À la carte: the lamb, which is sourced specifically. The pasta. The fish from the daily selection.

**Price range:** $$$–$$$$. Takapuna's upper tier — worth the occasion.

**The room:** A warm, considered dining room that holds a small number of tables and gives each one appropriate attention.

## Malt

**What it is:** A Takapuna bar and restaurant that has built its reputation on the intersection of a serious beer programme and food that exceeds bar kitchen expectations.

**Why it matters:** Malt has solved the problem that most craft beer bars have not: making the food as interesting as the drinks. The rotating tap list draws from New Zealand's best craft breweries. The menu is built to work with it — not an afterthought, but a kitchen that understands the pairing logic between hops and food.

**What to order:** Ask what is on tap this week — the list changes and the bar team can explain what is interesting. The food: the charcuterie, the bar plates, the main dishes from the kitchen.

**Price range:** $$. Bar pricing, good value for the quality.

**The room:** A Hurstmere Road venue with a layout that works for a group.

---

## Crave Café & Restaurant

**What it is:** A Takapuna all-day restaurant that functions as the suburb's serious neighbourhood option — the kind of place you eat at every month, not once a year.

**Why it matters:** Crave has earned its place in Takapuna's dining geography by being reliably excellent in a way that all-day restaurants rarely manage. The brunch is one of the best on the North Shore. The dinner, which gets less attention from food media, is equally worth it.

**What to order:** The brunch — whatever the kitchen is most excited about. The seasonal dinner menu. The baked goods and pastries, which are made in-house.

**Price range:** $$. North Shore neighbourhood restaurant value.

## The Lake House

**What it is:** A lakeside venue in the Takapuna area that provides one of Auckland's best outdoor dining settings for a casual meal with a view.

**Why it matters:** The Lake House occupies a position that few Auckland restaurants can match: a genuinely beautiful setting on the water that does not use the view as an excuse to coast on the food. The menu is seasonal and sourced from the region. The wine list is reasonably priced for the setting.

**What to order:** The fish of the day. The brunch menu in the morning. The outdoor setting on a summer afternoon.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Setting premium applied reasonably.

## How to Eat in Takapuna

**Walk Hurstmere Road.** The main dining street in Takapuna runs parallel to the beach and carries most of the suburb's restaurant options within a ten-minute walk.

**The beach before dinner.** Takapuna Beach is a genuine Auckland treasure — the pohutukawa at the northern end, the views across to Rangitoto. A walk before dinner at Ika or Estuary is one of Auckland's better evening rituals.

**Book for the serious venues.** Estuary and Ika fill on weekends. Book in advance. The North Shore's dining public is not underserved and the best restaurants are not undersold.

**Cross the bridge the other way.** North Shore locals cross to Ponsonby. Ponsonby locals should cross to Takapuna more than they do — the reciprocal recommendation is overdue.

Several Takapuna venues list midweek specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the North Shore's dining scene is building and the restaurants are motivated to fill their quieter nights with quality diners rather than empty seats.
