# Best Restaurants in Havelock North 2026

Havelock North is not a city. It is a village of 15,000 people at the foot of Te Mata Peak, twenty minutes from Napier. It should, by population logic, have café culture and a pub and nothing more. It has, instead, one of the most concentrated collections of excellent restaurants in New Zealand — a dining scene that punches at a level that cities five times its size do not consistently reach.

The explanation is wealth and agriculture. Havelock North is one of New Zealand's most affluent small towns, surrounded by Hawke's Bay's wine, olive, and food production. The population has money, has travelled, and has calibrated expectations. The restaurants respond accordingly.

---

## Craggy Range Winery Restaurant

**What it is:** The most important winery restaurant in Hawke's Bay — a Te Mata Peak setting, estate wines from one of New Zealand's most decorated wineries, and a kitchen that has earned national recognition.

**Why it matters:** Craggy Range has built its restaurant around the same philosophy as its wine: New Zealand can produce world-class product if it has the ambition and the patience to do it properly. The restaurant menu reflects the Hawke's Bay season and the winery's specific agricultural philosophy. The Le Sol Syrah and the Sophia Merlot Cabernet Franc are poured at the table where they make the most sense.

**What to order:** The seasonal tasting menu paired with Craggy Range estate wines. À la carte: the Hawke's Bay lamb, the seasonal game, whatever the kitchen received from the Te Mata region this week.

**Price range:** $$$$. Winery restaurant at the highest level.

**Book ahead:** Always. This is one of New Zealand's most sought-after dining reservations.

## Malo

**What it is:** Village Road's most celebrated restaurant — a Havelock North neighbourhood restaurant that produces food at a level that makes people drive from Napier.

**Why it matters:** Malo has built its following through consistent excellence and through a menu that reflects the Hawke's Bay produce geography with genuine attention. The seasonal approach is real — the menu changes with what is growing and what is available. The wine list gives serious space to Hawke's Bay's best producers. The room is warm and the service is engaged.

**What to order:** The seasonal specials — the kitchen is most interesting when interpreting what arrived from the Hawke's Bay farms this week. The lamb. The stone fruit desserts in summer.

**Price range:** $$$. Havelock North's best neighbourhood restaurant.

**Book ahead:** Weekend dinner needs a booking.

## The Store

**What it is:** A Havelock North café-deli that functions as the village's food hub — specialty coffee, artisan provisions, and café food made with the region's produce.

**Why it matters:** The Store has built its identity around being genuinely useful: the best coffee in the village, the best provisions for cooking at home (local olive oil, regional preserves, artisan cheese), and food made in the kitchen from ingredients that reflect Hawke's Bay's agricultural identity. The Saturday morning visit to The Store is a Havelock North ritual.

**What to order:** The specialty coffee. The cabinet food — made in-house from local produce. The provisions to take home: Hawke's Bay olive oil, local honey, seasonal fruit from the nearby orchards.

**Price range:** $$. Village café pricing.

---

## Pipi Brasserie

**What it is:** A Havelock North restaurant with a menu that takes the brasserie format seriously — European in structure, seasonal in content.

**Why it matters:** Pipi fills the Havelock North gap between the village café and the Craggy Range splurge — a genuinely good brasserie at a price point that makes it a regular rather than a special occasion. The wine list reflects the surrounding wine country. The food is consistently above what the price suggests.

**What to order:** The seasonal specials. The fish, which reflects the Hawke's Bay coast and the fishing community at Napier. The desserts, which are made in-house.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Brasserie pricing.

## Eating in Havelock North

**Pair with Te Mata Peak.** The walk or drive to the top of Te Mata Peak and back down to Craggy Range for lunch or dinner is one of New Zealand's great food-and-landscape combinations. The peak takes forty-five minutes to walk. Craggy Range is fifteen minutes from the top.

**The Village Road cluster.** Malo, The Store, Pipi, and several cafes and wine bars are all within a short walk of each other on and around Village Road. Havelock North is compact enough to explore on foot.

**Hawke's Bay wine country from the village.** The winery cellar doors of the Gimblett Gravels (fifteen minutes from Havelock North) are accessible by car. Craggy Range, Elephant Hill, Trinity Hill, and a dozen others make the region a full wine-tourism destination.

Several Havelock North restaurants list their midweek specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the village that fills on weekends with wine tourists often has its best value and most relaxed dining on midweek evenings.
