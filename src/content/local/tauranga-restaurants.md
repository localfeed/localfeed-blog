# Best Restaurants in Tauranga, Bay of Plenty 2026

Tauranga has been New Zealand's fastest growing city for the better part of a decade, and the dining scene is catching up with the population. What was a provincial city with provincial food aspirations five years ago has developed a restaurant culture that reflects the Bay of Plenty's extraordinary produce geography: kiwifruit, avocados, citrus, local seafood from the Bay, stone fruit from the surrounding orchards.

The chefs who have chosen Tauranga over Auckland or Wellington are finding something compelling: lower costs, a loyal local population that is growing rapidly, and access to produce that many New Zealand cities cannot match. The results are beginning to show.

---

## Elizabeth Café & Larder

**What it is:** Tauranga's most celebrated café-restaurant — a Devonport Road venue that has become the city's reference point for both serious brunch and serious dinner.

**Why it matters:** Elizabeth has done the difficult thing of building a venue that functions at its best across all meals. The brunch has become a Tauranga institution — the food is sourced from Bay of Plenty producers, the menu changes seasonally, and the kitchen treats brunch with the same seriousness as dinner. The evening service has followed the brunch reputation and justified the expansion.

**What to order:** The seasonal brunch plates — the eggs preparations use Bay of Plenty produce in ways that make the ingredients the point. At dinner, the seasonal menu with whatever the kitchen has received from local farms this week.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Tauranga's reference point for quality dining.

## The Clarence

**What it is:** A Tauranga fine dining restaurant that has given the city a room worth visiting for a significant occasion.

**Why it matters:** The Clarence has committed to being Tauranga's serious restaurant — tasting menu capability, a wine list with genuine depth, a kitchen that sources with intelligence. For a city that has historically required its fine dining visitors to drive to Auckland, the arrival of a restaurant at this level is significant.

**What to order:** The chef's selection for a full experience. À la carte: the Bay of Plenty seafood, which reflects the kitchen's understanding of what its geography makes possible. The wine list's New Zealand section.

**Price range:** $$$–$$$$. Tauranga fine dining.

## Harbourside Ocean Bar Grill

**What it is:** A Tauranga waterfront restaurant with Harbour Bridge views — seafood-focused, with access to Bay of Plenty fish and shellfish.

**Why it matters:** Harbourside has the best position in Tauranga's dining geography — the waterfront, the harbour views, the connection to the fishing industry that supplies the Bay. The seafood is genuinely fresh and the kitchen knows how to handle it. The setting makes it a natural venue for a special occasion.

**What to order:** The fresh fish of the day from the Bay of Plenty fleet. The shellfish — local scallops, mussels, and oysters when in season. The seafood platter for sharing.

**Price range:** $$$. Waterfront dining, well-priced for the experience.

## Macau

**What it is:** A Tauranga restaurant serving Macanese cuisine — a fusion of Portuguese and Chinese cooking traditions that is one of the most interesting and underrepresented cuisines in New Zealand.

**Why it matters:** Macau has brought something genuinely unusual to Tauranga: a cuisine with a specific cultural identity (the result of four hundred years of Portuguese influence on a Chinese colony) that is not well represented anywhere in New Zealand. The African chicken, the bacalhau preparations, the custard tarts that are the direct ancestor of the pastel de nata — this is a kitchen with a point of view.

**What to order:** The African chicken. The bacalhau (salt cod) preparations. The Macanese curry. The custard tarts to finish.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Well-priced for the cuisine's quality and rarity.

## Tay Street Beach Café

**What it is:** A Mount Maunganui beachside café — across the harbour from Tauranga proper, worth including for the setting and the quality.

**Why it matters:** The Mount Maunganui cafe and restaurant scene (fifteen minutes from Tauranga CBD) has developed alongside the suburb's status as one of New Zealand's most desirable beach locations. Tay Street is the best expression of what the beachside cafe format can be — the setting is exceptional, the food is genuinely worth ordering, and the coffee is from a serious roaster.

**What to order:** The brunch menu — the eggs and the seasonal plates that use Bay of Plenty produce. The coffee. The view of Mount Maunganui beach, which does not require an order.

**Price range:** $$. Beach café pricing.

## Eating in Tauranga and the Bay of Plenty

**Mount Maunganui is worth crossing the bridge.** The restaurant and cafe scene across the Tauranga Harbour Bridge in Mount Maunganui runs at a high level and has distinct energy from the Tauranga CBD. Budget time for both.

**The produce is the reason.** Bay of Plenty kiwifruit, avocados, and citrus appear in the region's better kitchens in ways you will not find in Auckland. The connection between the orchards and the plates is shorter here than anywhere else in the country.

**Te Puke is twenty minutes away.** The "kiwifruit capital of the world" is close enough for a day trip. The produce at the Tauranga farmers markets (Saturday, Tauranga CBD) reflects this geography directly.
