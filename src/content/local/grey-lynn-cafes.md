# Best Cafes in Grey Lynn, Auckland 2026

Grey Lynn's cafe culture is inseparable from the Saturday morning farmers market at the Grey Lynn Community Centre. The market — one of Auckland's best — draws a neighbourhood crowd that treats the surrounding cafes as the second act: market produce in one hand, flat white in the other.

The suburb's cafe scene reflects its demographic: educated, food-aware, opinionated about coffee, and uninterested in paying for spectacle over substance. The cafes that last here are the ones that earn the regulars rather than capturing the foot traffic.

---

## Richmond Road Eatery

**What it is:** The Grey Lynn neighbourhood cafe that defines what the suburb's cafe culture looks like at its most characteristic — serious coffee, excellent food, a room full of people who come every week.

**Why it matters:** Richmond Road Eatery has the qualities that distinguish a great neighbourhood cafe from a good one: the coffee is from a roaster who is doing interesting things, the food changes with what is fresh, and the room has genuine warmth rather than manufactured character. The Saturday morning crowd here is Grey Lynn in its most concentrated form.

**What to order:** The seasonal breakfast plates. The flat white, which is made correctly and consistently. The weekend brunch menu — the kitchen's most ambitious preparation of the week.

**Price range:** $$. Grey Lynn neighbourhood cafe pricing.

---

## Kokako

**What it is:** Auckland's most celebrated specialty roaster with a Grey Lynn cafe on Surrey Crescent — the best expression of what Kokako does as a roaster in a single venue.

**Why it matters:** Kokako has been at the top of Auckland's specialty coffee scene for over a decade. The Grey Lynn cafe is where you drink the roaster's work closest to source. The sourcing is ethical — direct trade, fair prices to farmers — and the quality reflects those relationships. The food programme is equally serious.

**What to order:** The pour-over from the current seasonal single-origin selection. The espresso. Ask which lot the baristas are currently most excited about. The cabinet food is made in-house.

**Price range:** $$. Specialty roastery cafe pricing.

**The room:** Surrey Crescent setting, a warm space that has become the reference point for the Auckland neighbourhood cafe aesthetic.

## Store

**What it is:** A Grey Lynn cafe-deli hybrid that operates as the neighbourhood food store, specialty coffee bar, and weekend brunch venue simultaneously.

**Why it matters:** Store has built its following by being useful in the fullest sense — a place to buy good coffee, good provisions, and eat well without it being formal. The deli counter is stocked with local and artisan producers. The coffee programme is serious. The brunch is worth travelling to the suburb for.

**What to order:** The cabinet food for a quick visit. The brunch plates for a longer one. Whatever is freshest in the deli for the way home.

**Price range:** $$. Neighbourhood deli-cafe pricing.

## No. 1 Coffee Bar

**What it is:** A Grey Lynn specialty cafe on Great North Road with a focused approach — excellent coffee, minimal menu, executed correctly.

**Why it matters:** No. 1 operates on the principle that doing a small number of things very well is better than doing many things adequately. The coffee is from a serious Auckland roaster, extracted with calibration. The food is limited and well-sourced. The room is small and usually full.

**What to order:** The espresso or the filter — whichever reflects how you want to drink the current roaster selection. The cabinet food for a simple accompaniment.

**Price range:** $$. Specialty cafe pricing.

## Grey Lynn Farmers Market: The Context

The Saturday Grey Lynn Farmers Market (Grey Lynn Community Centre, 8am–12pm) is the event that the suburb's cafe culture surrounds. The produce sold here — vegetables from Pukekohe and Kumeu farms, bread from local bakers, honey, preserves, cheese — is among the best available at any Auckland market.

The cafes listed above are all within walking distance. The correct Saturday morning sequence: arrive at the market, collect provisions, breakfast at Richmond Road Eatery or Kokako, return home with something interesting for the afternoon. This is how Grey Lynn Saturdays work for the people who live there.

## Coffee in Grey Lynn: What to Know

**Kokako is the roaster to know.** Grey Lynn's most important coffee contribution to Auckland is Kokako — a roaster that has been setting the standard for ethical specialty coffee in New Zealand for fifteen years. If you have not engaged with Kokako's single-origin offerings, the Grey Lynn cafe is the best place to start.

**Saturday morning needs a plan.** The best Grey Lynn cafes fill completely by 9:30am on Saturday. Arrive at 8am or 10:30am. The gap in between is the busiest thirty minutes in the suburb's week.

**The neighbourhood is walkable.** The cafes above, the Saturday market, and several of Grey Lynn's best restaurants (Cotto, Winona Forever) are all within twenty minutes on foot of each other.

Several Grey Lynn cafes list their quiet-weekday specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the neighbourhood that runs on Saturday mornings often has its best midweek offers for the work-from-home regulars who populate the suburb Tuesday through Thursday.
