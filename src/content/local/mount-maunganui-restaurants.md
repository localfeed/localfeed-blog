# Best Restaurants in Mount Maunganui 2026

Mount Maunganui is New Zealand's lifestyle suburb — the beach houses, the surf culture, the walk around the base of the mountain that every New Zealander has done at least once. The real estate prices reflect the lifestyle premium. So does the dining scene.

The Mount has developed a café and restaurant culture that is disproportionately excellent for its size, driven by the same affluent, active, food-aware population that has been paying premium prices for the lifestyle. The cafes have responded with specialty coffee. The restaurants have responded with serious kitchens. The result is a beachside suburb that eats better than most New Zealand cities.

---

## The Coronation

**What it is:** The Mount's most celebrated restaurant — a destination dining room that has given the suburb a restaurant worth travelling to from Tauranga and beyond.

**Why it matters:** The Coronation has committed to being a serious restaurant in a beach suburb — tasting menu capability, a wine list with genuine depth, a kitchen that sources from the Bay of Plenty and treats the region's produce as the point. The room is one of the best in the Bay of Plenty region.

**What to order:** The degustation or the seasonal à la carte. The Bay of Plenty produce — the kiwifruit, the avocado, the coastal seafood — appears throughout. The wine pairing.

**Price range:** $$$$. Mount Maunganui's finest.

**Book ahead:** Essential. The room is small and it fills.

---

## Macau

**What it is:** Macanese cuisine — the fusion of Portuguese and Chinese cooking traditions — in a Mount Maunganui setting that has become the suburb's most interesting restaurant.

**Why it matters:** The Mount's dining public is adventurous enough to support a restaurant with a genuinely unusual cuisine identity, and Macau has earned its following. The African chicken is the dish that brings people back. The Portuguese egg tarts are the best version in New Zealand.

**What to order:** The African chicken. The bacalhau. The piri piri prawn. The egg tarts to finish.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Well-priced for the cuisine.

---

## The Hub

**What it is:** A Mount Maunganui café and restaurant that has become the suburb's neighbourhood institution — the kind of place that functions across all meals and all occasions.

**Why it matters:** The Hub has built its following by being reliable and excellent rather than spectacular and inconsistent. The brunch is among the best in the Bay of Plenty region. The coffee is from a serious roaster. The dinner is worth coming specifically for.

**What to order:** The seasonal brunch plates using Bay of Plenty produce. The flat white. At dinner: the seafood and the seasonal specials.

**Price range:** $$. The suburb's neighbourhood pricing at above-neighbourhood quality.

---

## Tay Street Beach Café

**What it is:** A beachfront café with one of the best settings in New Zealand café culture — Tay Street, directly opposite the beach.

**Why it matters:** Tay Street has earned its setting rather than coasting on it. The food is genuinely made in the kitchen, the coffee is from a serious roaster, and the brunch menu changes seasonally. The beach view is a constant.

**What to order:** The brunch menu — seasonal plates, eggs made correctly. The flat white on the terrace with the beach visible. The cabinet food for a quick visit.

**Price range:** $$. Beach café pricing.

**The room:** The terrace overlooking the beach. The correct table order: the one closest to the sand.

---

## Haché

**What it is:** A Mount Maunganui burger restaurant that has become the suburb's most consistent quick-meal destination.

**Why it matters:** Haché has taken the quality burger format seriously — New Zealand beef, in-house sauces, proper buns — in a suburb that could coast on its lifestyle appeal. The result is a burger worth ordering on its merits.

**What to order:** The classic beef burger, which is the reference point for the kitchen's sourcing and technique. The chicken option. The fries.

**Price range:** $$. Honest burger pricing.

## Eating at the Mount

**Walk to the beach first.** The walk around the base of the mount — forty-five minutes — is the correct pre-dinner activity. It contextualises the lifestyle that the restaurants are serving.

**The Tauranga connection.** The Mount and Tauranga CBD are fifteen minutes apart across the harbour. The combined dining scene is stronger than either suburb independently — Elizabeth Café & Larder (Tauranga) and The Coronation (the Mount) together represent the Bay of Plenty dining at its peak.

**Summer weekends need booking.** The Mount's population in December and January swells significantly. The Coronation and Macau in particular need advance bookings for summer weekend dinner.

**The café culture is the daily rhythm.** The Mount's most characteristic eating experience is not dinner at The Coronation — it is Saturday morning coffee at Tay Street, the queue at The Hub, the flat white on the walk back from the mountain.
