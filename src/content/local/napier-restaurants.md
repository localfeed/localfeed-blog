# Best Restaurants in Napier, Hawke's Bay 2026

Hawke's Bay is New Zealand's wine country in the fullest sense — not just vines, but a food and produce culture that runs through the orchards of the Heretaunga Plains, the olive groves outside Havelock North, the artisan producers scattered across the region. It is the only part of New Zealand where visiting the wine region and eating excellently are the same trip.

Napier is the base. The Art Deco city on the water has a dining scene that reflects its wine-country geography: restaurants sourcing locally as a matter of course, wine lists that give serious space to Hawke's Bay producers, chefs who moved here for the ingredients.

---

## Bistronomy

**What it is:** Napier's finest restaurant — a tasting menu and à la carte operation that has become the dining destination for Hawke's Bay wine region visitors.

**Why it matters:** Bistronomy occupies a position in Napier that Amisfield occupies in Central Otago: the restaurant that integrates the wine region into its identity completely. The menu is built around Hawke's Bay and New Zealand produce. The wine list gives extraordinary space to local producers. The kitchen executes with a precision that makes it worth travelling from Auckland for, not just visiting while already in the region.

**What to order:** The tasting menu with Hawke's Bay wine pairing. The à la carte: the lamb, the beef, and whatever stone fruit or seasonal produce is running as a dessert course.

**Price range:** $$$–$$$$. The destination restaurant of the region — priced accordingly.

**Book ahead:** Always. Visitors to the wine region who do not book in advance find Bistronomy full.

---

## Mister D

**What it is:** Emerson Street dining — a Napier restaurant that has built one of the city's most interesting menus at a price point that makes it a genuine regular rather than a special occasion.

**Why it matters:** Mister D has taken the comfort food concept seriously in a way that very few restaurants manage: the food is familiar in format (fried chicken, burgers, cocktails) and genuinely excellent in execution. The locally sourced ingredients make the difference. The craft cocktail programme is among the best in the Hawke's Bay region.

**What to order:** The fried chicken. The beef burger with local beef. The cocktails — the list changes and the bartenders know it. The ice cream, made in-house.

**Price range:** $$. Among the best value for quality in Napier.

**The room:** Emerson Street location, buzzy, a room that works for every occasion from lunch to late drinks.

---

## Pacifica

**What it is:** A Marine Parade restaurant with harbour views and a menu that reflects Napier's coastal position — seafood-forward, seasonal, well considered.

**Why it matters:** Pacifica has made the combination of Napier's Art Deco setting and its ocean position into a dining proposition. The seafood is sourced from the region's fishing community. The wine list is weighted toward Hawke's Bay white wines — the pairing logic makes sense for the food. The room in summer, with the marine parade visible, is one of the best dining settings in the North Island.

**What to order:** The daily seafood — whatever came in from the local fleet. The crayfish when in season. The Hawke's Bay wines by the glass, which allow you to work through the region's diversity.

**Price range:** $$$. Destination restaurant pricing, appropriate for the experience.

---

## Elephant Hill Winery Restaurant

**What it is:** A clifftop winery restaurant in Te Awanga, thirty minutes from Napier, with one of New Zealand's most extraordinary dining settings and a wine list built from the estate's production.

**Why it matters:** Elephant Hill is Hawke's Bay wine tourism at its peak — a spectacular clifftop setting above the coast, estate wines poured at the source, and a kitchen that has been serious about the food for long enough to build a genuine reputation independent of the view. The Syrah and Chardonnay are exceptional. The food matches them.

**What to order:** The seasonal menu, designed around what the Hawke's Bay kitchen garden and regional suppliers are producing. The wine pairing — the winery's team selects for the menu and the knowledge of what is in the bottle is unsurpassable. The lamb.

**Price range:** $$$$. Destination winery restaurant. Plan the visit as the event.

**The setting:** Te Awanga clifftop. The drive from Napier is part of the experience.

---

## The Crown Hotel

**What it is:** A Napier heritage hotel restaurant that has become a reliable city-centre dining option — the kind of hotel restaurant that operates as a genuine restaurant, not a captive audience service.

**Why it matters:** The Crown Hotel's dining room occupies one of Napier's Art Deco interior spaces and has invested in a kitchen that takes the setting seriously. The menu is regional — Hawke's Bay lamb, local produce, Hawke's Bay wines — and the execution is consistent.

**What to order:** The lamb. The seasonal specials. The wine list's regional section, which has good selections at multiple price points.

**Price range:** $$$. Hotel restaurant pricing, reasonable for the quality.

## Eating in Hawke's Bay: The Full Picture

**Napier is the base, Havelock North is worth the drive.** Havelock North (twenty minutes from Napier) has its own dining scene centred on Village Road — Malo, Craggy Range Winery Restaurant, and a cluster of good cafes and wine bars that make it worth an afternoon.

**Craggy Range Winery Restaurant** is one of the best winery restaurants in New Zealand — the Te Mata Peak setting, the Gimblett Gravels wines, and a kitchen that has won significant recognition nationally. If you are in Hawke's Bay and do not visit, you missed something.

**The food producers are accessible.** Hawke's Bay's farmers markets, the Hawke's Bay Food and Wine Trail, and the willingness of the region's producers to welcome visitors make Napier a food-and-wine destination in the complete sense. Build your visit around both.

**Book the cellar doors and the winery restaurants together.** Elephant Hill and Craggy Range in particular need advance table reservations. Their wine tastings can often accommodate walk-ins, but the restaurants will be full on a summer weekend.

Several Napier and Hawke's Bay restaurants list their midweek specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the shoulder season and midweek tables at the region's best dining rooms are accessible for those who plan ahead.
