# Best Bars in Christchurch 2026

The 2011 earthquakes destroyed Christchurch's bar geography as comprehensively as they destroyed everything else. The Oxford Terrace bar strip — "The Strip" — was condemned and demolished. What replaced it was not a reconstruction but a reinvention.

The post-earthquake bar scene grew in new locations, in new formats, in new buildings. Container bars, warehouse conversions, garden bars in places that used to be office buildings. The generation of bar owners who opened in the rebuild years did so into an opportunity: no inherited landlords, no legacy expectations, a city that was literally rebuilding and open to new ideas. The results are worth visiting.

---

## Pomeroy's Old Brewery Inn

**What it is:** Christchurch's most serious craft beer bar — a Victorian brewery building in St Asaph Street that has been converted into the city's anchor for people who take beer seriously.

**Why it matters:** Pomeroy's has built its reputation on what craft beer bars should do: a rotating tap list from the country's best breweries, a bottle and can selection that goes further than the taps, a beer garden that functions properly, and a bar team that knows the difference between what they are pouring. The food is pub food executed correctly — pies and burgers from a kitchen that uses good ingredients.

**What to order:** Ask what is freshest on the Canterbury and national taps. The seasonal special releases when available. The bar food: the pies, the burgers.

**Price range:** $$. Craft beer bar pricing.

**The room:** Victorian brewery converted into a bar and garden. One of Christchurch's most distinctive bar settings.

## Civil & Naval

**What it is:** A Victoria Street cocktail bar and café that functions as Christchurch's most considered cocktail room.

**Why it matters:** Civil & Naval has built its cocktail programme around the same sourcing intelligence as its café operation — seasonal ingredients, locally made spirits where possible, the Canterbury craft distilling scene represented. The bar team knows classic technique and applies it with the seasonal focus that makes the list interesting.

**What to order:** The seasonal cocktail from the current menu. A classic cocktail made from the Canterbury spirits selection. The natural wine list for those not in the cocktail mood.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Cocktail bar pricing.

## Volstead Trading Company

**What it is:** A Christchurch prohibition-era themed cocktail bar with a serious spirits programme and the most theatrical setting of any bar in the post-rebuild city.

**Why it matters:** Volstead has built its identity around the 1920s American prohibition era — the Volstead Act that banned alcohol — and has used that identity to construct a bar programme that takes pre-prohibition cocktails and their revival seriously. The rye whiskey programme is the most complete in Christchurch.

**What to order:** The pre-prohibition classics — the Old Fashioned, the Sazerac, the Sidecar. The rye whiskey neat, from the selection the bar team recommends. Ask about what is interesting in the American spirits section.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Cocktail bar pricing.

---

## Smash Palace

**What it is:** A Gloucester Street bar in a converted shipping container garden — one of Christchurch's most beloved post-earthquake venues and still one of its best.

**Why it matters:** Smash Palace was born from the earthquake opportunity — a vacant lot, a collection of shipping containers, a garden bar format that the city had not tried. The result has been one of Christchurch's most enduring venues. The craft beer selection is good. The garden setting is exceptional. The informal atmosphere is the right antidote to the more polished venues.

**What to order:** Whatever is on the craft beer taps. The bar food — simple and honest. The garden on a Christchurch summer afternoon, which is one of the city's most characteristic experiences.

**Price range:** $$. Garden bar pricing.

---

## Cassels & Sons Brewery

**What it is:** A Christchurch craft brewery and bar in the Woolston area — on-site brewing, a large garden, and food from a kitchen that takes the pub role seriously.

**Why it matters:** Cassels has built one of New Zealand's most complete brewery-bar experiences: the beers are brewed on-site (visible through the brewery windows), the range spans from session beers to experimental releases, and the garden and food programme make it a destination for an afternoon rather than a quick pint.

**What to order:** The Cassels house range at source — the Amber, the Stout, the seasonal releases. The garden setting. The bar food from the kitchen.

**Price range:** $$. Brewery bar pricing.

## Drinking in Post-Rebuild Christchurch

**The geography has moved.** The old Oxford Terrace bar strip is gone. Christchurch's bar geography is now distributed across Victoria Street, the Terrace precinct, Woolston (Cassels), and the scattered garden bars that occupy empty lots around the city. Research before you go.

**Lyttelton for the alternative.** The Lyttelton bar scene (fifteen minutes over the Port Hills) is Christchurch's most interesting after-dark district — the London Street bars have more character per metre than anything in the CBD.

**The rebuild created garden bars.** Christchurch's post-earthquake landscape of empty lots produced a generation of garden bar culture — Smash Palace, several others — that is now a defining feature of the city's drinking identity.

Several Christchurch bars list their midweek specials on [LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) — the city rebuilding its social infrastructure uses offers to build the regular-drinking culture that takes time to establish.
