# Best Brunch in Wellington 2026

Wellington's café culture is well documented. What is less documented is that the brunch extends beyond weekend scrambled eggs — Wellington eats seriously at brunch on weekdays in a way that most New Zealand cities do not, driven by a public service and university population that works flexibly and eats accordingly.

The brunch culture here is demanding. Wellington's café public has calibrated expectations from decades of serious coffee culture, and the kitchen standards have followed. These are the brunch venues that justify the Wellington reputation.

---

## Fidel's Cafe

**What it is:** Cuba Street, going since 1997. The shakshuka is the most ordered brunch dish in Wellington.

**Why it matters:** Fidel's brunch has achieved something rare: a specific dish (the shakshuka) that is so correctly made that it has become the reference point for the city. The eggs are cooked in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce that is genuinely made — not from a jar, not approximated. The coffee is from a serious roaster. The room is Cuba Street at its most characteristic.

**What to order:** The shakshuka. The eggs on house-baked toast. The cabinet baking — whatever came out of the oven that morning.

**Price range:** $$. Cuba Street's consistent fair pricing.

---

## Loretta

**What it is:** A Cuba Street bistro with a brunch programme that has become one of the most followed in Wellington.

**Why it matters:** Loretta's brunch goes beyond the eggs-and-toast format. The kitchen produces plates that reflect genuine creativity: seasonal produce used intelligently, preparations that make the ingredients the point, food that is worth ordering even if you have eaten brunch a thousand times and thought you were over it.

**What to order:** The brunch boards — the combination platters that let you explore the menu laterally rather than committing to a single plate. The seasonal vegetable preparations. The pastry from the cabinet.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Well-positioned for the quality.

---

## Prefab Cafe

**What it is:** A specialty coffee operation with a brunch programme that takes the kitchen as seriously as the roaster.

**Why it matters:** Prefab has built its following through consistency and through a menu that changes with genuine attention to what is seasonal and what is local. The coffee is roasted in-house. The brunch food reflects the same sourcing ethic as the beans.

**What to order:** The seasonal brunch plates. The batch brew coffee alongside. The pastry — whatever the baker produced that morning.

**Price range:** $$. Specialty café pricing.

---

## Rūaumoko

**What it is:** One of Wellington's most followed café openings of recent years — specialty coffee and genuinely creative brunch food.

**Why it matters:** Rūaumoko has taken the Wellington specialty café template and added a food programme that matches it. The brunch menu is built from what is local and seasonal rather than a fixed format. The kitchen produces plates that are worth coming to the café specifically for, not just alongside the coffee.

**What to order:** Whatever the kitchen is leading with this week. Ask the staff what they are most proud of on the current menu.

**Price range:** $$. Specialty café.

---

## The Hangar

**What it is:** A Wellington waterfront café with one of the city's best positions — harbour views, the promenade, a brunch menu that earns the location.

**Why it matters:** The Hangar does not rely on its setting to excuse mediocre food. The brunch menu is seasonal and carefully sourced. The coffee is from a serious roaster. The location — the waterfront promenade near Frank Kitts Park — makes it the right place for a long Saturday morning.

**What to order:** The eggs preparations that change with the season. The brunch plates from the seasonal menu. The flat white. The outdoor terrace for the harbour view.

**Price range:** $$. Waterfront café pricing.

---

## Golding's Free Dive

**What it is:** Cuba Street bar-café that runs a serious coffee and food programme in the morning hours.

**Why it matters:** Golding's morning service — before the bar switches into evening mode — is one of the better café experiences on Cuba Street. The coffee is from independent specialty roasters. The food is made with care. The room is quieter in the morning than it will ever be again in the day.

**What to order:** The coffee from the current specialty roaster. The cabinet food — the pastries and the savoury cabinet options made that morning.

**Price range:** $$. Good value.

## Wellington Brunch: When and How

**Weekdays are better.** Wellington eats brunch on weekdays in a way that makes Tuesday at 9am a completely different experience from Saturday at 10am. The same cafes, a fraction of the queue, a more relaxed room.

**The public service brunch window.** Wellington's government workers take their breaks seriously. The 9:30am–11am window on weekdays fills good cafes quickly — arrive at 9am or 11:30am.

**Loretta on Saturday needs patience.** The weekend Loretta queue is real. Either book (they do take bookings for weekend brunch), arrive when they open, or accept the wait as part of the Wellington brunch ritual.

**Walk the Cuba Street precinct.** Fidel's, Loretta, Golding's, and Rūaumoko are all within a ten-minute walk of each other. Wellington brunch is a pedestrian activity.
