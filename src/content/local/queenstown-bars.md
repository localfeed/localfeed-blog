# Best Bars in Queenstown 2026

Queenstown's bar scene has a volume problem. The lakefront and The Mall carry a density of bars designed for tourist throughput — shots, après-ski, the kind of late night that ends somewhere regrettable. That scene exists and is not this guide.

This guide is about the bars worth drinking in. The places with actual wine programmes, actual cocktail knowledge, actual beer selections chosen by someone who cares. They exist in Queenstown and they are findable if you know where to look.

---

## The Bunker

**What it is:** A bar and restaurant accessed through a door off The Mall — the entry point for one of Queenstown's most atmospheric rooms, with a cocktail programme that matches the setting.

**Why it matters:** The Bunker's underground log-fire room has the best atmosphere in Queenstown by a significant margin. The cocktail list is serious — the bartenders have knowledge of classic technique and apply it — and the spirits selection reflects investment rather than convenience. The restaurant above (technically the same venue) is equally excellent, which is rare.

**What to order:** A spirit-forward cocktail — Manhattans, Negronis, Sazeracs are done correctly here. The whisky selection is deep. The amaro list for after dinner.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Queenstown cocktail bar pricing.

**The room:** Below street level, stone walls, the log fire burning through winter. Arrive before it fills.

---

## Sherwood

**What it is:** A Queenstown hotel bar on the edge of town that has become one of the most interesting drinking destinations in the South Island — craft beer and natural wine focus, a terrace looking at the Remarkables.

**Why it matters:** Sherwood has built its identity around a specific kind of Queenstown experience: unhurried, connected to the landscape, quality-focused. The beer programme pulls from New Zealand's best craft breweries. The wine list is natural and biodynamic, selected with intelligence. The food that comes from the kitchen functions as genuine bar food rather than an afterthought.

**What to order:** The rotating craft beer taps — ask what is local. The natural wine section. The bar food: the pizzas from the wood-fired oven are legitimately excellent.

**Price range:** $$. One of the most honest-value drinking experiences in Queenstown.

**The setting:** The terrace with Remarkables views in the afternoon is one of the South Island's genuine drinking pleasures.

---

## Minus 5° Ice Bar

**What it is:** A novelty bar kept at minus five degrees where everything — the glasses, the furniture — is made of Fiordland ice.

**Why it matters:** Listed here because Queenstown is partly about experiences you do not have elsewhere, and Minus 5° qualifies. The drinks are not the point. The experience — genuinely cold, genuinely made of ice — is. Go once, bring a date who has not been, understand it as theatre.

**What to order:** Whatever comes with your entry package. The cocktails are not the reason.

**Price range:** $$$ (entry fee plus drinks).

---

## Atlas Beer Cafe

**What it is:** A Beach Street bar with one of Queenstown's most serious craft beer selections — a genuine beer destination rather than a venue that happens to have beer.

**Why it matters:** Atlas has built its reputation on curation: the taps rotate through New Zealand craft breweries with a focus on small-batch and interesting styles. The understanding of what is on the list is reflected in the floor team's ability to explain it. The food is bar food done correctly — it does not distract from the beer but holds up alongside it.

**What to order:** Ask what is freshest from a New Zealand craft producer. The seasonals when available. The bar food: the burgers and the bar plates.

**Price range:** $$. Craft beer bar pricing.

---

## Zephyr

**What it is:** A Queenstown bar with a natural wine and craft cocktail focus — a quieter option than the lakefront venues, more serious in its drinks programme.

**Why it matters:** Zephyr occupies the small but growing Queenstown category of bars that understand wine and cocktails without being pretentious about it. The natural wine selection rotates. The cocktail list reflects some creativity rather than the tourist-capture minimum.

**What to order:** The natural wine of the week — ask what has arrived recently. The cocktails from the seasonal menu.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Evening bar pricing.

## Drinking in Queenstown: The Honest Guide

**Avoid The Mall at midnight.** The lakefront strip after 11pm is a different experience from what this guide covers. If that is what you are after, it is there. If you want bars with actual quality, the venues above are the ones.

**The ski season changes everything.** July and August Queenstown is a different city from January Queenstown. The après-ski culture is genuine, the après-ski crowds are enormous. The Bunker, Sherwood, and Atlas hold their quality through the ski season — arrive early, book if you can.

**Sherwood is worth the ten-minute drive.** It is not on the lakefront. The taxi or the walk is worth it for the terrace, the beer, and the absence of tourist-volume noise.
