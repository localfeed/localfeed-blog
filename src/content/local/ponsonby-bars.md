# Best Bars in Ponsonby, Auckland 2026

Ponsonby Road transforms after 5pm. The brunch crowd clears, the kitchen prep gives way to service prep, and the strip shifts into the version of itself that runs until midnight. It is one of Auckland's best bar streets — dense enough to walk between venues, varied enough that the night can take several directions.

The bars on this list are not interchangeable. Each has a character. Knowing which one you want before you arrive is the Ponsonby move.

---

## Apero

**What it is:** Auckland's best natural wine bar — a Ponsonby institution that takes wine as seriously as anywhere in New Zealand.

**Why it matters:** Apero has built its reputation on a wine list that is consistently one of the most interesting in the country: natural, biodynamic, and skin-contact wines from producers in Europe, Australia, and New Zealand, with a floor team that knows the list well enough to make you care about each recommendation. The food — small plates, kitchen-serious — is the best bar food on the strip.

**What to order:** Ask what is exciting on the list this week. The floor team will steer you toward whatever has just arrived and is worth your attention. The food: the charcuterie, the cheese, and whatever the kitchen is running tonight.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Natural wine pricing is what it is. The list justifies it.

**The room:** Small, warm, not loud. The right bar to start or end an evening with people you want to talk to.

---

## SPQR

**What it is:** A Ponsonby Road institution — an Italian-themed bar and restaurant on the corner that has been one of Auckland's favourite rooms since 1991.

**Why it matters:** SPQR's longevity comes from doing things correctly: a serious cocktail and Negroni programme, Italian and Italian-adjacent food that punches above bar food standards, and a room that generates energy without becoming unpleasant. The Aperol Spritzes and Campari drinks are made properly. The arancini are made in-house.

**What to order:** A Negroni or Aperol Spritz to start. The arancini. The pizza, which is better than it needs to be from a bar kitchen. The amaro selection at the end of the night.

**Price range:** $$. Bar pricing that is fair for the quality.

**The room:** Two levels, the downstairs bar and the upstairs dining room. The bar on a Friday evening is a specific Auckland experience — crowded, loud, excellent. The upstairs is where you go for dinner.

## The Tuning Fork

**What it is:** A Ponsonby cocktail bar with a music focus — named for the connection between the bar programme and live music that has shaped its identity.

**Why it matters:** The Tuning Fork occupies the serious cocktail category without the snobbery that often accompanies it. The bartenders know what they are doing and the menu reflects current cocktail culture — seasonal ingredients, precise technique, interesting spirits selection — without being inaccessible.

**What to order:** Ask the bartender what they are currently proud of. The cocktail menu changes and the regulars know to ask rather than order from the printed list. The whisky selection is well considered.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Auckland cocktail bar pricing.

---

## Mea Culpa

**What it is:** A Ponsonby wine bar with a serious Spanish and European wine focus, and bar food that takes the kitchen seriously.

**Why it matters:** Mea Culpa operates in the growing Auckland category of wine bars that treat food as part of the offer rather than an afterthought. The Spanish wine focus — Rioja, Ribera del Duero, Albariño, sherry — gives it a point of view that differentiates it from the natural wine bars and the cocktail bars. The jamón is excellent. The anchovies are the right anchovies.

**What to order:** Explore the Spanish wine section — ask for help if you are not familiar with the region. The jamón platter. The cheese. The pintxos if they are running them.

**Price range:** $$–$$$. Wine bar pricing, reasonable for what you get.

---

## Bedford Soda & Liquor

**What it is:** A Ponsonby cocktail bar in the Ponsonby Central complex with a focus on spirit-forward cocktails and a well-considered beer programme.

**Why it matters:** Bedford occupies the Ponsonby Central evening ecosystem (alongside Azabu and Little Bird's evening service) and serves the pre-dinner and post-dinner drinking crowd from the complex. The cocktail programme is more serious than the casual setting suggests.

**What to order:** The spirit-forward cocktails — Manhattans, Old Fashioneds, Sazeracs — if you are in that mode. The craft beer selection if not. The bar food from the kitchen.

**Price range:** $$. Ponsonby Central positioning.

---

## Golden Dawn

**What it is:** A Ponsonby venue that exists in multiple categories simultaneously — bar, restaurant, live music venue, and the kind of place that Auckland's creative industries have been drinking at for fifteen years.

**Why it matters:** Golden Dawn solved the problem that many Auckland bars have: how to be a place for a weeknight drink, a weekend dinner, and a live music night, without doing any of them poorly. The bar programme is solid. The food from the kitchen is better than a venue this size and this range usually manages. The music programme has introduced Auckland to international artists before they became famous.

**What to order:** Whatever is on tap — the beer selection changes. The food: the pizzas and the bar plates. Stay for the music if anything is on.

**Price range:** $$. One of Ponsonby's best-value bars.

**The room:** Rambling, covered outdoor space, a layout that works for small groups in multiple configurations.

## Drinking in Ponsonby: How It Works

**The strip is walkable.** Apero to SPQR to Golden Dawn to the venues around Ponsonby Central is a walkable evening. You are never far from the next option.

**Book a dinner reservation before you start drinking.** Ponsonby's best restaurants (Orphans Kitchen, Gemmayze Street, Azabu) do not hold tables. If you want to eat seriously after drinks, book in advance — do not try to walk in after several glasses of natural wine.

**The 5pm–7pm window is underrated.** Ponsonby's bars at early evening are a different experience from 10pm: quieter, the bartenders are less stretched, and you can actually talk. Apero in particular is better at 5:30pm than at 9pm.
