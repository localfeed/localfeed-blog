December is when most NZ restaurants make between 15–25% of their annual revenue. The four weeks before Christmas concentrate the corporate events, family gatherings, end-of-year celebrations, and group bookings that drive the highest revenue weeks of the year.

Venues that plan for this period from September fill their capacity and execute well. Venues that start planning in November are already behind.

## The Christmas Booking Timeline

**September**: Open your Christmas booking calendar. Publish your Christmas menus on your website. Corporate event organisers, who are the highest-value bookings (full-room or large private dining), are planning in September and October.

**October**: Your Christmas page should be live. Your Christmas set menus should be finalised and priced. Your minimum spend for Christmas group bookings should be established and communicated clearly.

**November**: Begin direct marketing to your customer list about Christmas bookings. The message: availability is going fast, here is what we have on, here is how to book. Send this early in November, not in the last week.

**First two weeks of December**: Final push for remaining availability. Focus on your email list and Instagram for smaller group bookings (2–6 people) that can fill remaining slots.

## The Christmas Set Menu

Almost every NZ restaurant that handles Christmas groups runs a set menu for the period. The advantages: predictable kitchen load, better food cost management, faster service for large groups.

**Pricing the Christmas menu**: Christmas menus in NZ typically run 20–35% above your regular average spend per head, reflecting the premium occasion and the guaranteed revenue. A venue with a $65 average dinner cover typically prices their Christmas set menu at $85–$95 per head.

**What to include**: Three courses, a welcome drink, and optional beverage packages. Pre-ordering for groups of 8+ is standard — it makes service manageable and reduces kitchen complexity on a busy night.

**Minimum numbers and minimum spend**: A Christmas table of 12 should have a minimum spend requirement. State it clearly at booking. This protects you from the group of 12 that orders minimal food and maximum house wine.

## Corporate Groups: The Highest-Value Christmas Segment

Corporate end-of-year events represent the largest individual bookings in December. A company Christmas dinner for 30–50 people is a significant revenue event.

The corporate Christmas booking window opens in August–September. Venues that are not positioned by September are competing for the slots other venues do not have.

How to win corporate Christmas business:

**Have a clear private dining or full-venue-hire offering**: Corporates want to know exactly what space they are getting, the catering format, the pricing, and what is included. A clear proposal template (capacity, set menu options, minimum spend, AV or room setup capabilities) removes friction.

**Respond to enquiries same day**: The corporate event market is competitive in NZ. The first venue that responds clearly and professionally often wins the booking regardless of who else was contacted.

**Direct outreach in September**: An email to local business contacts (via LinkedIn, direct email lists, or professional networks) saying "our Christmas calendar is now open for corporate events — here is what we have available" generates enquiries from people who had not yet started searching.

## Deposit and Cancellation Policy for Christmas

Christmas is when no-shows and last-minute cancellations have the highest commercial impact. A group of 20 that does not show on December 20th is a significant revenue loss that is very difficult to fill at short notice.

Christmas bookings should require:
- A deposit of $20–$30 per person at booking to confirm
- A clear cancellation policy (100% deposit forfeited within 14 days of the event)
- Full pre-payment for groups above a certain size, with the balance settled on the night

These terms are standard and well-understood in the NZ market. State them clearly and follow through. Venues that enforce their policies consistently have significantly lower Christmas no-show rates.

## After Christmas: Capturing the January Trade

The customers who dined with you in December — whether corporate groups or individual tables — are the warmest prospects for January and beyond. Capture their contact details during December and follow up in mid-January with something personal.

"Thank you for spending part of your December with us. We are back open from the 13th and January is genuinely one of our favourite months — quieter, more relaxed, and a good time to come and have a proper dinner without the festive rush."
