The NZ cafe POS market has consolidated significantly. Most operators are choosing between four or five serious options, all of which are cloud-based, iPad-operated, and integrated with standard accounting and payment tools. The difference is not the feature list — most of them have comparable features. The difference is the reporting depth, the support quality, and the total cost of ownership when you include hardware, monthly fees, and transaction rates.

## What a NZ cafe POS actually needs to do

Before comparing options, establish what you need from the system. For a NZ cafe, the non-negotiables are:

**Fast item lookup at the counter**: in a busy morning service, the POS needs to surface the right item in under two taps. A cluttered interface that requires searching costs you time at the one moment when time is most expensive.

**Kitchen display or print integration**: orders going to the kitchen need to be clear and sequenced correctly. A POS that cannot handle concurrent orders to multiple prep stations is not suitable for a cafe doing more than 40 covers in the morning rush.

**Accurate daily reporting**: end-of-day sales by category, voids and refunds, staff performance if you have more than one team member. These numbers are your primary financial management tool and the POS is where they live.

**Inventory management or cost tracking**: not every cafe needs full stock management in the POS, but item cost data that informs your food cost percentage tracking is worth having.

**Integration with your accounting software**: automatic export to Xero or MYOB saves 3–5 hours per week in manual data entry and eliminates a category of human error. This is close to non-negotiable for any cafe doing more than $10,000 per week in revenue.

**STAT:** $4,200/year · Average time cost of manual accounting entry for a NZ cafe not integrated with accounting software, calculated at $25/hour for data reconciliation and error correction. POS-to-Xero integration pays for itself in most venues within three months.

## The options most NZ cafes are choosing

This is not an exhaustive comparison — the market moves and pricing changes. But these are the four systems most commonly used in NZ cafes in 2026:

**Lightspeed Restaurant**: strong reporting, good inventory management, higher price point at the subscription level. Better suited to multi-location businesses or venues with complex menus than to a simple cafe operation.

**Square for Restaurants**: lower monthly cost, free hardware in some configurations, strong payment integration. Reporting is less detailed than Lightspeed. Suitable for a simpler cafe operation with straightforward menu structures.

**Kounta/Lightspeed (via Kounta)**: NZ-built, good local support, strong integration with Xero. Has been absorbed into the Lightspeed ecosystem. Still used by many NZ cafes that built their workflow around it.

**OrderMate**: Australia and NZ focused, strong kitchen display integration, suitable for higher-volume hospitality environments. Less suited to smaller simple cafe operations.

**H&L**: New Zealand and Australian market focus, long-established, good support network. Feature-rich but the interface can be less intuitive than newer iPad-native systems.

> The best POS for your NZ cafe is the one your team can use accurately at 8am during the morning rush, not the one with the longest feature list in the sales demo.

## The total cost calculation NZ cafe owners miss

Monthly subscription cost is visible. The total cost of ownership is less obvious.

For any NZ cafe POS, the full cost includes:

- Monthly subscription (typically $50–$200/month depending on the system and feature tier)
- Hardware: iPad ($800–$1,200), cash drawer ($150–$300), receipt printer ($400–$600), card reader ($100–$300 or included in some configurations) — total hardware is often $2,000–$3,000 for a single terminal setup
- Transaction fees: most cloud POS systems integrate with a specific payment processor. Transaction fees of 1.4%–2.4% on card revenue are significant at volume. On $30,000 monthly card revenue, a 0.5% difference in transaction fee is $150/month, $1,800/year.
- Training time: switching POS systems costs 2–4 weeks of reduced productivity while the team learns the new interface. Factor this into any switch decision.

**NOTE:** When comparing POS options, calculate the total annual cost including hardware amortisation over three years, monthly subscription, and transaction fees on your actual monthly card revenue. That number is the real comparison point, not the headline subscription price.

## The integration question

Your POS does not exist in isolation. The integrations that matter for NZ cafes:

**Xero or MYOB**: automatic daily journal entries, sales by category, and reconciliation against your bank feed. Essential for any cafe serious about financial management.

**Booking and reservation systems**: if you take table bookings, a POS that connects to your booking tool (or at minimum allows easy lookup of reservations) reduces errors and improves the handoff from booking to service.

**Loyalty programmes**: if you run a loyalty programme beyond a simple punch card, the POS needs to record customer visits and spending.

**Online ordering**: if you take online orders for pickup or delivery, the POS should receive those orders directly rather than requiring manual re-entry.

[LocalFeed](https://localfeed.co.nz) bookings are currently managed separately from POS systems, but the booking confirmation and customer data it captures complement the daily reporting your POS provides — together they give you a more complete view of your cover sources and customer behaviour than the POS alone.

**FACT:** Zero commission on food revenue. $10/week after 20 bookings. 75% of no-show fees go to the venue.

The POS decision is a four-to-five year commitment in most cases — switching is expensive in time and money. Get the decision right by calculating total cost of ownership honestly, trialling the shortlisted options during an actual service, and asking for references from NZ cafes of a similar size and complexity to yours before signing.
