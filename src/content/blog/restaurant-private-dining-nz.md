A private dining room is one of the most underutilised assets in NZ hospitality. Venues spend significant money building out a private space, then fail to market it deliberately, and watch it sit empty on Tuesday and Wednesday nights while the main floor struggles.

Private dining is not a side offering. Done correctly, it is a distinct revenue stream with better economics than regular table service.

## Why Private Dining Has Better Economics

**Higher minimum spend**: Private dining bookings almost always carry a minimum spend requirement. A group of 12 with a $1,500 food and beverage minimum is guaranteed revenue before a single dish is plated.

**Set menus and beverage packages**: Private dining groups accept set menus far more readily than individual tables. This reduces kitchen complexity, reduces food waste, and improves food cost percentage. A three-course set menu at $75 per head, costed carefully, delivers better margins than a fully à la carte service.

**Groups book on any day**: Corporate events, birthday dinners, and team celebrations are driven by the group's calendar, not by whether it is a weekend. A Thursday corporate dinner in your private room is full-margin revenue on a session that might otherwise be your quietest.

**Advance payment or deposit**: Well-structured private dining bookings require a deposit or full advance payment. This eliminates no-shows, smooths cash flow, and converts the booking into a guaranteed revenue event.

## Building a Private Dining Product

Before marketing private dining, the product needs to be clearly defined:

**Minimum spend or minimum numbers**: What is the minimum that justifies staffing and kitchen preparation for a private event? Be explicit and list it clearly. Ambiguity around minimums creates uncomfortable conversations at booking.

**Set menu options**: Offer two or three set menu options at different price points. A simple two-course option, a three-course standard, and a premium four-course with matched wines covers most corporate and social occasion needs.

**Beverage packages**: A drinks package (beer, wine, non-alcoholic at a set price per head) simplifies ordering and improves beverage revenue. Groups find packages easier to pre-plan and pay for.

**Hire fee vs minimum spend**: For daytime exclusive use (meetings, product launches, corporate away days), a room hire fee is appropriate in addition to or instead of a food and beverage minimum.

## Marketing Private Dining

The most significant failure in NZ private dining is under-marketing. The private dining page is buried in the navigation, has no clear pricing, and generates no enquiries.

**Make it findable on Google**: A dedicated private dining landing page, with explicit copy about capacity, minimum spend, and the contact process, captures searches for "private dining Auckland" or "venue for private dinner Wellington." These are high-intent searches from people actively looking for exactly what you offer.

**Corporate outreach**: The corporate sector is the highest-value segment for weekday private dining. A direct email to executive assistants and office managers within 2km of your venue — introducing your private dining offering with clear pricing and a simple booking process — generates enquiries that no amount of Instagram posts will.

**LinkedIn**: For venues targeting the corporate market, a LinkedIn presence with periodic posts about your private dining space and events reaches a professional audience that is specifically the buyer of corporate dining experiences.

**Local event planning community**: Event planners and corporate travel coordinators book private dining regularly. Building relationships with two or three local event planners creates a recurring referral channel.

## The Enquiry Process

The most common failure point in private dining is the enquiry experience. A potential group booker reaches your website, cannot find pricing, submits a generic enquiry form, and receives a response three days later. They have already booked somewhere else.

Optimise the enquiry process:

**Clear pricing on the website**: Not exact, but a range. "Private dining from $65 per head, minimum 10 guests." This pre-qualifies enquiries and gives the prospect enough information to decide whether to proceed.

**Fast response**: Target same-business-day response to private dining enquiries. These bookers are often managing multiple options simultaneously.

**Simple booking confirmation**: A brief proposal email with date, format, pricing, and a deposit link. The simpler the confirmation, the faster the booking closes.

## What to Do After a Successful Private Event

Private dining groups are repeat bookers. A company that does their end-of-year dinner at your venue in December is a warm prospect for next year's event in August. A family that celebrates a 50th birthday dinner in your private room is a warm prospect for the next milestone celebration.

Capture every private booking contact. Follow up six months later. The lifetime value of a repeat private dining client is significant.
