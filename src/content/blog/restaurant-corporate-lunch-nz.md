A corporate lunch table of four at full margin on a Tuesday is worth more to your weekly P&L than a discount table of four on a Saturday. The Saturday table was probably coming anyway. The Tuesday table required you to earn it — but the economics once you have it are superior.

Corporate lunch business is one of the most underexploited commercial opportunities in NZ restaurant operations. Here is how to build it.

## Who Is Actually Buying Corporate Lunches

Corporate lunches in NZ happen for specific reasons:

**Client entertainment**: Professional services firms (law, accounting, consulting, finance) regularly take clients to lunch. These lunches are expensed, which means price sensitivity is lower than personal dining.

**Internal team meals**: End-of-quarter lunches, new hire welcomes, leaving parties, team recognition. The organiser is usually a manager or EA who needs a reliable venue that will not let them down.

**Business development**: Two professionals meeting over lunch to discuss a potential relationship. Usually two people, modest spend, but regular frequency among the professional population.

**Board and executive dining**: Higher-end, smaller groups, significant spend per head, strong preference for privacy and discretion.

Each of these has slightly different needs. The core requirements that apply to all: reliability, professional atmosphere, efficiency (back within the hour if needed), and a booking process that does not require significant effort from the organiser.

## Building the Corporate Lunch Product

Before marketing corporate lunches, define what you are actually offering:

**The lunch menu**: A specific lunch menu, separate from your dinner menu if possible, priced for the midday occasion. Two courses at $42–$52 per head, or a set menu option at a fixed per-head price for groups, are the formats that work for corporate dining.

**Time commitment**: Corporate diners often need to be back in the office within 90 minutes. A set menu or a well-paced à la carte lunch that commits to an 80-minute service is commercially important. Communicate it. "We can have you back in the office within 90 minutes" is a selling point.

**Private or semi-private space**: For groups of 4 or more, private or semi-private space significantly increases the appeal. If your venue has any separable section, position it explicitly for corporate bookings.

**Easy invoicing**: Corporates need an invoice for their expense claim. Having a simple process for providing a GST invoice to a business name is basic but often not set up. Fix this.

**Direct booking contact**: A corporate EA researching venues will send an email or make a phone call. If the response takes 48 hours, they have already booked elsewhere. A same-day response to corporate enquiries is essential.

## How to Market to the Corporate Audience

**Direct outreach**: The most effective marketing for corporate lunch in NZ is direct contact with the people who book corporate lunches — executive assistants, office managers, senior professionals. A personal email to the top 20–30 businesses within 1km of your venue, introducing your corporate lunch offer and including a menu and contact details, generates responses.

Do this once per quarter. The EA who was not booking corporate lunches when you first emailed may be in a quarter with three client lunches scheduled.

**LinkedIn presence**: Your venue's LinkedIn page, posting about corporate dining capability, reaches the professional audience that buys it. A LinkedIn post about your private dining offer, tagged to your suburb, is seen by professionals in your area.

**Google Business Profile**: Include "business lunch" and "corporate dining" in your profile description and service categories. Some searches for corporate venues start with Google.

## The Corporate Account Relationship

Once you have a corporate client booking regularly, treat them as the asset they are:

- Know the booker by name and respond to them personally
- Keep a record of preferences and requirements so they do not have to repeat them
- Be proactive about suggesting the venue for their next event before they have to think of it
- Acknowledge when their team has been going through a busy period and express appreciation

A corporate client who books four lunches per quarter at $400–$600 per booking is $1,600–$2,400 in quarterly revenue from one relationship. The relationship management cost is minimal.
