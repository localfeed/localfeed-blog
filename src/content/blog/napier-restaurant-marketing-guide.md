Napier and Hawke's Bay operate on a hospitality model that differs from most NZ cities. The region is a tourism destination as much as a place where people live. The wine industry, the Art Deco architecture, the Mission Estate, and the beach strip create a visitor economy that drives hospitality revenue in a way that Hamilton or Palmerston North simply do not have.

That creates specific marketing opportunities and specific challenges.

## Understanding Hawke's Bay Diner Types

**Tourists and visitors**: The majority of discretionary dining in Napier comes from out-of-towners, particularly during summer, harvest season, and the Art Deco festival. These diners are high-intent and willing to spend.

**Wine trail visitors**: The Hawke's Bay wine trail brings people who are specifically food-and-wine motivated. They spend more per head, are receptive to provenance stories, and tend to visit multiple venues in one day.

**Locals**: The Napier-Hastings combined population of around 80,000 creates a local regular base. Less tourist-driven but essential for midweek viability.

## The Tourism Marketing Channels

For Napier venues with tourist-facing positioning, the channels that matter most:

**TripAdvisor**: Significantly more important for Napier than for most NZ cities. Tourists from Australia, the UK, and North America use TripAdvisor more than locals do. If your venue appears on the first page of Napier restaurant results and has strong reviews, you capture tourist traffic that locals would never find you through.

**Google Business Profile**: Essential. "Dinner Napier" and "restaurants Hawke's Bay" are high-intent search queries with real conversion. Fully populate your profile, post weekly, respond to reviews.

**Wine trail partnerships**: If your venue is positioned near the wine trail corridor, explicit partnerships with wineries — appearing on their "where to eat" recommendations, being mentioned in winery newsletters — can deliver high-quality diner referrals.

**Tourism New Zealand and Hawke's Bay Tourism**: Getting onto official tourism recommendation lists and the HB Tourism website is worth pursuing. These are warm referrals from a trusted authority.

## The Harvest Season

Hawke's Bay's harvest season (roughly February–April) creates a specific hospitality surge. The influx of seasonal workers, the increase in wine-related visitors, and the local celebratory energy around harvest create a natural peak.

Venues that plan explicitly for harvest — harvest menus, special events, collaborations with wineries for harvest dinners — build revenue and reputation during the peak that carries through quieter months.

## The Off-Season Problem

January and harvest aside, Napier has a genuine off-season hospitality problem. The city becomes significantly quieter from May through August. Venues that built their business model around tourist traffic struggle.

The strategies that work for off-season:

**Locals first**: The local Napier-Hastings population is your revenue base when tourists are not present. Build a direct relationship with local diners year-round rather than only focusing on the tourist peak.

**Corporate and events**: The HB business community has its own events calendar. Corporate lunches, end-of-year dinners, and business events happen in the off-season. Make it easy for local businesses to book your venue for groups.

**Direct marketing**: An email list of locals and returning visitors (people who visited last summer and gave you their contact details) is the off-season fill mechanism. One well-written email in June can fill a Thursday night that would otherwise be empty.

## The Art Deco Festival and Events

Napier's Art Deco festival (February) creates one of NZ's strongest single-event hospitality spikes. Venues that plan for the festival — advance bookings open months ahead, event-specific menus, festival-themed promotions — extract maximum value from the city's strongest marketing moment.

Beyond Art Deco, the Mission Concert series and various Hawke's Bay Wine Celebration events create recurring peaks worth planning around.

## What Works in Napier

The strongest Napier venues in 2026:

- Have both a tourist strategy (TripAdvisor, winery partnerships, tourism listings) and a local strategy (email list, community presence)
- Plan explicitly for the harvest peak and the winter off-season
- Build their local regular base as the business foundation beneath the tourist uplift
- Tell the Hawke's Bay provenance story (local wine, local produce) because it is genuinely compelling and differentiating
- Use the region's strong food-and-wine reputation as a marketing asset

Napier has one of the strongest regional hospitality identities in New Zealand. Venues that align themselves with that identity and communicate it clearly have a genuine competitive advantage.
