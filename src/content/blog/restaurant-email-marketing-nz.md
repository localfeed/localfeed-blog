A restaurant with a genuine email list of 500 engaged local customers can fill a quiet Tuesday with one email. No paid advertising. No platform commission. No discounting. Just a direct message to people who have already told you they like eating at your venue.

Most NZ restaurants have no email list. Most of the ones that do have one do not use it. This is one of the most significant missed opportunities in NZ hospitality.

## Why Email Outperforms Social Media for Restaurants

The instinctive channel for restaurant marketing in 2026 is Instagram. The better channel is email.

**Reach**: An Instagram post reaches 3–8% of your followers organically. An email to your list reaches 35–50% of recipients (typical open rates for hospitality).

**Intent**: Someone on your email list explicitly gave you their contact details and permission to message them. That is a warmer audience than a follower who may have clicked follow two years ago and scrolled past your content since.

**No algorithm dependency**: Email is yours. Instagram can change its algorithm and eliminate your organic reach overnight. Email lists cannot be taken from you.

**Direct to booking**: An email with a direct booking link drives bookings in a way that an Instagram post requires multiple steps to replicate (DM to book, profile link, platform redirect).

The only advantage Instagram has is discoverability — new customers who do not know you yet. For nurturing the customers who already know and like you, email is the superior channel.

## Building Your Email List

The list must be built with explicit permission. Signing people up without asking is both bad practice and, in New Zealand, potentially a breach of the Unsolicited Electronic Messages Act.

**Sources of permission:**

**Booking system**: Every diner who books through your own system has provided contact information. Add a checkbox at booking: "Subscribe to receive occasional emails from us about specials and events." Most customers say yes if the framing is right.

[LocalFeed](https://localfeed.app) provides venue owners with customer contact data from every booking. This is your list growing every week without any additional effort.

**Paper signup at the venue**: A simple card at the table or at the register: "Join our mailing list for early access to specials and events: [short URL or QR code]." Low-tech but converts well in hospitality settings where people are having a good time.

**Events and private dining**: Every event booking is an opportunity to add multiple email addresses to your list from a group that has already demonstrated they enjoy your venue.

**Website signup**: A simple email capture on your website with a genuine reason to sign up: "Join our list for monthly specials, seasonal menu updates, and early access to our events."

## What to Send

The fatal mistake: signing up for email marketing, asking a designer to build an expensive template, writing it three times, and never sending. The perfect email never sent is worth zero. A plain-text email that goes out once a month is worth a great deal.

**What works:**

**Monthly venue update**: What is new on the menu. What has changed. What is worth coming in for this month. Short, personal, from the owner or chef. No template required.

**Quiet session fill**: "If you want to have dinner this week without fighting for a table, Tuesday and Wednesday are the nights. Come in and actually enjoy your evening." This reframe — quiet sessions as a better experience, not a discount — works. It does not require a discount to drive bookings.

**Events and specials**: Wine dinners, tasting events, seasonal menus, local collaborations. Announce to your list first. Make them feel like insiders with early access.

**Occasional honest content**: "Here is what is actually hard about running a restaurant right now." NZ diners have a genuine interest in the hospitality world and respond warmly to authenticity. The occasional honest email builds the relationship without requiring a promotional angle.

**What does not work:**

- Weekly emails (fatigue kills your open rate)
- Heavily designed templates that look like corporate newsletters
- Constant discount offers (trains your list to only open emails with deals)
- Generic content that could be from any restaurant

## The Frequency Rule

Monthly is the minimum. Twice per month is optimal for most NZ venues. More than twice per month risks increasing unsubscribes.

The exception: event-driven emails can be sent more frequently when the content is relevant and time-sensitive. An email on Thursday about a Friday event has a purpose. An email on Thursday because it has been a week since your last email does not.

## The ROI Calculation

A list of 400 subscribers. Monthly email with a 40% open rate (160 people). 10% book as a result (16 covers). At $65 average spend, that is $1,040 in monthly revenue from one email.

The cost: 30–60 minutes to write and send. The email platform: $20–$50/month for Mailchimp or equivalent.

No channel in NZ hospitality has a comparable return on investment.
